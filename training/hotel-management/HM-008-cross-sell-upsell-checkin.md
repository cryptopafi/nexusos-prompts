# Cross-Selling and Upselling Protocol at Check-in

## Obiectiv
Systematically increase ancillary revenue per guest by training front office and F&B staff to present relevant upgrade and add-on offers at every touchpoint — targeting a minimum 20% upsell conversion rate at check-in, 25% breakfast/dinner package attachment rate, and an incremental ancillary revenue of 50–100 RON per occupied room night above baseline.

## Pași

### Pas 1: Preparation Before Guest Arrives — The Pre-Assignment Upsell Audit
- Review the arrivals list the evening before (or by 08:00 on the arrival day). For each arrival, conduct a 30-second upsell opportunity audit:
  - **Room upgrade opportunity**: is the guest in a Standard room and is a Superior/Deluxe room available? Calculate the upsell delta (the additional revenue if the guest accepts). Note it on the arrivals list: "Room 3 → Room 7 upgrade, delta = +80 RON/night."
  - **F&B package opportunity**: does the guest have breakfast included? If not, flag for breakfast upsell. Does the guest have dinner reserved? If not, flag for dinner upsell. For multi-night stays without any F&B package, the dinner upsell is the highest-value opportunity.
  - **Occasion flag**: is there a birthday, anniversary, honeymoon, or celebration noted in the reservation or guest profile? If yes, flag for a personalized package upsell (wine + flowers + room decoration, 150–250 RON).
  - **LOS extension opportunity**: is the guest arriving Thursday or Sunday for a 1-night stay? A length-of-stay extension ("Would you consider staying an extra night? We can offer the second night at 15% off") captures otherwise empty room nights.
  - **Experience opportunity**: is the guest a first-time visitor to the area? Flag for activity/experience upsell (guided hike, cooking class, wine tasting, local tour).
- Pre-calculate every upsell delta and note it on the arrivals list or in the PMS upsell module. The front desk agent should never need to look up prices during the guest interaction — every number must be at their fingertips.
- Load available add-on offers into PMS as one-click sellable items so the agent can add them to the folio instantly upon guest acceptance.

### Pas 2: The Upsell Menu — Know What to Offer and at What Price
- Create a comprehensive, tiered upsell menu. Every front desk agent must memorize this menu. Laminate a quick-reference card and place one at each front desk station.
- **Tier 1 — Room Upgrades** (highest revenue impact):
  - Standard to Superior: "+X RON per night — you get a larger room with [specific benefit: garden view, balcony, premium bathroom amenities]."
  - Superior to Deluxe: "+Y RON per night — [specific benefit: separate living area, premium view, fireplace]."
  - Any room to Suite (if available): "+Z RON per night — our best room with [specific unique features]."
  - Upgrade pricing rule: the upsell price should be 50–70% of the rack rate difference between room types. Example: if Standard is 300 and Deluxe is 450 (difference = 150), offer the upgrade at 80–100 RON (53–67% of the difference). The guest perceives a deal; you capture incremental revenue from a room that would otherwise go unsold at the higher tier. Never offer free upgrades during upsell conversations — it devalues the product.
- **Tier 2 — F&B Packages** (highest conversion rate):
  - Breakfast add-on: "Our breakfast features fresh local products, eggs to order, and seasonal fruit. It's X RON per person — shall I add it to your stay?" Conversion target: 30%+ for non-inclusive bookings.
  - Dinner reservation with preview: "Tonight our chef is preparing [describe the special]. Dinner is approximately X RON per person with wine pairing. Shall I reserve a table at 19:30?"
  - Half-board package: for multi-night stays, offer a bundled breakfast + dinner rate that is 10% below individual purchase. "For your 3-night stay, I can add breakfast and dinner every evening for X RON per person per night — that saves you about 15% compared to booking each meal separately."
  - Tasting menu experience: "We have a special fire-kitchen tasting menu with 7 courses paired with local wines — it's X RON per person and it's truly a once-in-a-lifetime experience. Shall I book you in?"
- **Tier 3 — Convenience Add-ons** (low resistance, easy conversion):
  - Late checkout: "Standard checkout is at 11:00. Would you like a late checkout at 14:00? It's X RON and gives you a relaxed morning." Conversion target: 15–20%.
  - Early check-in: "Your room is actually ready now — would you like to check in early for X RON?" (only offer if the room is genuinely ready).
  - Airport/station transfer: "Would you like us to arrange a transfer from [location]? It's X RON each way."
  - Parking (if charged): "We have secure parking available for X RON per night."
- **Tier 4 — Experience Packages** (premium revenue, emotional appeal):
  - Romantic package: "Bottle of local wine, rose petals, and candlelit turndown service for X RON — perfect if you're celebrating something special."
  - Cooking class: "Tomorrow morning our chef hosts a traditional Romanian cooking class — you cook alongside him over the open fire. It's X RON per person."
  - Nature experience: "We offer a guided sunrise hike / foraging walk / mountain bike ride — X RON per person including [specific inclusion]."
  - Spa / wellness (if available): "A relaxing massage after your hike is X RON for 60 minutes."
- Every offer must have a specific, stated price. Vague offers ("we can arrange something special") create hesitation and reduce conversion.

### Pas 3: The Upsell Conversation — The ABOE Script Framework
- Train every front desk agent on the ABOE framework (Anchor, Benefit, Offer, Easy Exit):
  1. **Anchor**: use the guest's name, acknowledge their booking, and establish a warm connection first. "Welcome, Mr. Ionescu! I have your reservation for 3 nights in our Classic Room — great choice." Complete the hospitality greeting before any upsell attempt. A guest who feels sold-to before they feel welcomed becomes defensive.
  2. **Benefit-led introduction**: lead with the benefit and the sensory/emotional appeal, NOT the price. "I wanted to let you know — we actually have our Garden View room available tonight. It's a bit larger, has a beautiful view of the forest, and the morning light through those windows is something our guests always comment on."
  3. **Offer with specific price**: state the price clearly, simply, and as the last element (after the benefit is established). "It's just 80 RON more per night for your stay." Use "just" or "only" to frame the price as accessible. Use "per night" not "total" unless the total is small — "240 RON total for 3 nights" sounds more expensive than "80 RON per night."
  4. **Easy exit**: never pressure. Give the guest a graceful way to decline without awkwardness. "I wanted to mention it in case you'd like the extra space — but your Classic Room is also lovely and it's fully ready for you." If they decline, smile genuinely. Move to the next upsell smoothly.
- After a room upgrade decline, transition to a cross-sell: "No problem at all! While I get your keys ready — shall I book a table for dinner tonight? Our chef has a special tasting menu this evening."
- After a dinner decline, offer the lower-resistance item: "Completely understood. How about our breakfast? It features fresh local products and eggs to order — X RON per person."
- **The cascade principle**: start with the highest-value offer (room upgrade), then cascade downward through F&B, then convenience add-ons. Stop after the second decline — pushing beyond two offers feels aggressive. Log the offers attempted and outcomes.

### Pas 4: Handling Objections — Prepared Responses
- Every common objection has a trained response. Agents who are not prepared for objections will freeze or back off immediately:
  - **"It's too expensive"**: "I completely understand. The upgrade is more of a treat — but we also have [lower-cost alternative]. Would a late checkout be useful? It's just X RON and gives you a relaxed morning."
  - **"I'm only here one night"**: "That's exactly why I thought it might be worth it — make the most of the one night! But if you'd prefer to keep it simple, your room is perfect. Can I book you a table for dinner instead?"
  - **"I already have dinner plans"**: "Great! In that case, would a late checkout be useful tomorrow? Gives you until 14:00 at your own pace." Or: "Before you go out, can I offer a drink at our bar? Our house cocktail is on us as a welcome."
  - **"We're on a budget"**: "Absolutely understood — no pressure at all. Can I add the breakfast for you? It's X RON and saves you the hassle of finding somewhere in the morning."
  - **"I need to ask my partner/group"**: "Of course! I'll hold the offer for you. Just let me know by [time] and I'll take care of it."
  - **"Can I get it for free?"**: "I'm not able to offer a complimentary upgrade today, but I can offer it at a special in-house rate of X RON — which is significantly less than the difference on our website. Would you like me to go ahead?"
- **Never repeat the same offer after a clear "no."** A guest who says no twice and is asked a third time becomes irritated. Log the declined offer, move on, and make the guest feel valued regardless of their purchase decision.

### Pas 5: Pre-Arrival Digital Upsell (Email and WhatsApp)
- **3 days before arrival**: send a pre-arrival email or WhatsApp message with 2–3 curated upsell offers.
  - Subject line: "Your stay is coming up, [Guest Name] — here's how to make it unforgettable"
  - Content: brief, visual, with clear pricing and a one-click booking button or reply prompt.
    - Offer 1: Room upgrade (photo + price + benefit).
    - Offer 2: Dinner experience (photo of a signature dish + price + "Book your table now").
    - Offer 3: Experience add-on (cooking class / hike / wellness — seasonal rotation).
  - Include a direct reply mechanism: "Simply reply to this email with your preference and we'll arrange everything."
- **Conversion benchmarks**: pre-arrival email upsell converts at 8–12% for room upgrades and 15–20% for F&B/experience packages. This is incremental revenue before the guest arrives — zero operational effort during the stay.
- **Automation**: set up through PMS email triggers (Cloudbeds, Mews support this natively) or through Mailchimp/Brevo with a booking date trigger. Once set up, the pre-arrival upsell runs automatically for every arriving guest.
- **WhatsApp alternative**: for properties in markets where WhatsApp is the primary communication tool (Romania qualifies), a personal WhatsApp message from the property 2 days before arrival is even more effective than email. "Hello Mr. Ionescu, this is [Name] from [Property]. We're looking forward to your arrival on Saturday! Would you like me to reserve a table for dinner? Our chef has a special fire-kitchen tasting menu this weekend."

### Pas 6: In-Stay Cross-Selling Opportunities
- Upselling does not stop at check-in. Every guest interaction during the stay is a cross-sell opportunity:
  - **At breakfast**: "How was everything this morning? Tonight we have a special tasting menu — shall I reserve a table for you?"
  - **At the restaurant**: "I notice you enjoyed the lamb — we also do a cooking class where you prepare it yourself over the fire. Would you be interested for tomorrow?"
  - **By housekeeping**: when a guest mentions they are going on a walk, housekeeping or front desk can suggest the guided version.
  - **At the bar**: "Since you're enjoying the local wines, we have a wine tasting experience available — would you like to learn more?"
  - **At checkout (for multi-night stays)**: "Would you like to extend your stay? We have availability tomorrow and I can offer the same room at [rate]."
- The key principle: in-stay cross-selling is contextual and conversational. It should feel like a helpful suggestion from a knowledgeable host, not a sales pitch. The trigger is the guest's expressed interest or behavior, not a script.

### Pas 7: Recording, Tracking, and Incentivizing Performance
- Every upsell attempt must be logged, either in the PMS upsell module or in a daily Upsell Log (Google Sheets):
  - Columns: Date | Guest Name | Room | Agent Name | Offer Presented | Price | Outcome (Accepted/Declined) | Revenue Generated | Notes.
- **Daily metrics per agent**:
  - Upsell attempt rate: number of attempts / number of check-ins. Target: 100% (every eligible check-in receives at least one offer).
  - Upsell conversion rate: accepted / attempted x 100%. Target: 20%+ for room upgrades, 30%+ for F&B (lower-resistance item).
  - Average upsell revenue per check-in: total upsell revenue / total check-ins.
  - Revenue per shift: total upsell revenue generated during the agent's shift.
- **Weekly team metrics**: display on a visible board (not in a spreadsheet nobody checks). Weekly leaderboard motivates competitive improvement.
- **Incentive program**: the agent who generates the most upsell revenue in a month receives a bonus. Structure options:
  - Flat bonus: 500 RON to the top performer.
  - Commission-based: 5% of all upsell revenue generated by the agent (directly aligns incentive with revenue).
  - Non-monetary: preferred shift scheduling, hotel restaurant dinner, employee of the month recognition.
- Announce results at the weekly team meeting. Celebrate top performers publicly. For underperformers, provide coaching — listen to their upsell interactions, identify specific improvement points, role-play scenarios.

### Pas 8: Revenue Impact Analysis and Optimization
- Monthly analysis: compare ancillary revenue per occupied room night this month vs. last month and vs. STLY.
  - Upsell revenue as a % of room revenue. Industry benchmark: a well-executed upsell program generates 5–12% incremental revenue above room revenue.
  - Which offers convert best? If room upgrades convert at 25% but late checkout converts at only 5%, investigate — is the late checkout price too high? Is the staff not offering it consistently?
  - Which agents convert best? If Agent A converts at 30% and Agent B at 8%, have Agent A mentor Agent B. Listen to their check-in interactions to identify the difference.
- **Seasonal offer adjustment**: rotate the experience offers by season.
  - Summer: outdoor activities (hiking, biking, swimming), sunset picnic packages.
  - Autumn: wine harvest experience, foraging walk, seasonal tasting menu.
  - Winter: fireplace room upgrade (if applicable), cooking class, Christmas/New Year special package.
  - Spring: garden room with wildflower view, outdoor breakfast option, nature photography walk.
- **Pricing optimization**: if an upsell converts at >40%, the price is probably too low — you are leaving revenue on the table. Increase the price by 10–15% and test. If an upsell converts below 10%, the price may be too high or the benefit is not compelling — adjust the offer or the pitch.

### Pas 9: Common Mistakes and Anti-Patterns
- **Mistake #1 — Upselling before welcoming**: the ABOE framework starts with Anchor (welcome) for a reason. A guest who feels sold-to before they feel welcomed will decline everything and leave with a negative impression. Welcome first. Upsell second.
- **Mistake #2 — Offering without enthusiasm**: "Would you like an upgrade? It's 80 lei extra." (Flat voice, no eye contact, reading from a script.) vs. "I've got to tell you — our Garden View room is actually available tonight, and the sunset from that balcony is honestly one of the best things about this property. It's just 80 lei more — would you like it?" Enthusiasm sells. Train agents to genuinely believe in the value of what they are offering.
- **Mistake #3 — Offering items the guest already has**: checking the reservation is step 1. If breakfast is included, do not offer to "add breakfast." If they have already booked dinner, do not offer dinner. This makes the agent look uninformed.
- **Mistake #4 — Giving away upgrades for free**: complimentary upgrades should be a strategic service recovery tool (HM-009) or a VIP recognition gesture, not a default because the agent feels bad charging. Every free upgrade is lost revenue and sets an expectation that upgrades are negotiable.
- **Mistake #5 — Not logging declined offers**: declined offers are valuable data. If 90% of guests decline the cooking class, maybe the price is wrong, the description is unclear, or the timing does not work. You cannot optimize what you do not measure.

## Note
- **Revenue impact illustration**: a 10-room property at 65% occupancy (2,373 room nights/year) with an average upsell revenue of 50 RON per occupied room night generates 118,650 RON in incremental annual revenue. At 80 RON per room night (achievable with strong F&B upsell), it is 189,840 RON. This is revenue with near-zero incremental cost — the room upgrade costs nothing (the room would be empty), the dinner ingredient cost is 30% of the sell price, and the late checkout costs nothing.
- **The psychology of upselling**: guests are not annoyed by well-delivered upsell offers. Research consistently shows that guests appreciate being informed about available options — provided the offer is relevant, the benefit is clear, and the exit is easy. What annoys guests is pressure, repetition after a decline, and insincerity.
- **Albastru / Sapte Focuri context**: the fire-kitchen dinner experience is the single most premium and emotionally compelling upsell available. Train every front office agent to describe it with genuine passion: "Our chef cooks everything over an open fire in traditional Romanian clay pots — it's a truly unique experience that our guests talk about for months. The tasting menu is X RON per person with wine pairing, and I honestly think it's the best thing about staying here. Shall I reserve your table?" This emotional, personal description converts at 2–3x the rate of a flat "dinner is available." For the corporate segment: upsell the team cooking experience ("Your team can cook alongside the chef over the open fire — it's the most memorable team-building activity we offer"). For couples: upsell the romantic package ("Bottle of wine, candlelit dinner by the fire, and breakfast in bed — X RON"). For families: upsell the kid-friendly cooking class or the nature activity package. Match the upsell to the segment for maximum conversion.

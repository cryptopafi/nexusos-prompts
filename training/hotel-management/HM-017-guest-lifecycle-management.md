# Guest Lifecycle Management (Pre-arrival to Post-departure)

## Obiectiv
Design and execute a consistent, personalized guest journey across 6 phases — from booking confirmation to post-departure loyalty — maximizing satisfaction, ancillary revenue, repeat booking rate (target: 25%+ of guests return within 18 months), and positive review generation (target: 1 review per 3 stays). The lifecycle approach treats every guest interaction as a deliberate touchpoint that builds toward loyalty, not a random encounter.

## Pași

### Pas 1: Phase 1 — Booking Confirmation (within 5 minutes of reservation)
- **Trigger**: reservation confirmed in PMS (all channels — direct, OTA, phone, email).
- **Automated confirmation email** (via PMS or email marketing tool):
  - Clear booking summary: dates (with day of week), room type and name, rate and what is included (breakfast Y/N, parking Y/N, dinner Y/N), total cost, payment terms, cancellation policy with dates.
  - Property details: full address, GPS coordinates (critical for rural properties where street addresses are unreliable), driving directions from major cities (Bucharest: X hours via E81, Pitesti: X minutes via DN7), nearest public transport option.
  - Check-in time and policy: "Check-in is from 15:00. Early check-in may be available — let us know and we'll do our best to accommodate."
  - 3–5 experience highlights: build anticipation. "During your stay, don't miss: (1) Dinner at Sapte Focuri — our chef cooks everything over an open fire in traditional clay pots (book your table in advance!), (2) The sunrise view from the terrace, (3) Our guided nature walk through the surrounding forest."
  - Direct contact: "Any questions? Reply to this email, call us at [number], or message us on WhatsApp at [number]. We're here to help."
  - Tone: warm, personal, not corporate. "We're genuinely looking forward to welcoming you" not "Your reservation has been confirmed."
- **For OTA bookings**: the PMS should auto-generate an internal confirmation. Send the guest a supplementary email (if the OTA provides the email address — Booking.com does for non-Genius bookings) with the experience highlights and direct contact. This begins the OTA-to-direct conversion journey.
- **Internal action**: flag any special requests or occasion notes for downstream departments. If the reservation mentions a birthday or anniversary, create a task for housekeeping to prepare the room amenity.

### Pas 2: Phase 2 — Pre-Arrival (3 days before check-in)
- **Pre-arrival email** (automated, personalized by name and stay details):
  - Subject: "3 days until your stay, [Guest Name] — here's everything you need to know"
  - Content:
    - Warm greeting and excitement: "We're looking forward to having you!"
    - Upsell offers (see HM-008 Pas 5): room upgrade option, dinner reservation prompt, experience booking (cooking class, guided hike, wine tasting). Include photos and specific prices. One-click booking links or simple "Reply YES to book."
    - Practical information: parking (where to park, is it free?), nearest ATM and pharmacy, nearest fuel station (for rural properties), weather forecast for the stay dates (shows you care about their experience), what to pack if relevant ("bring comfortable walking shoes for our forest trails").
    - Check-in time reminder with option to request early check-in.
    - Direct contact details.
  - **Conversion benchmarks**: pre-arrival emails convert at 8–12% for upgrades and 15–20% for experience add-ons. At 20 RON average upsell per converted email, a property with 2,000 arrivals/year generates 4,000–8,000 RON in pre-arrival upsell revenue — fully automated.
- **For VIP and repeat guests** (identified from PMS guest profile):
  - Replace the automated email with a personal note from the GM or property owner. WhatsApp is ideal: "Hello Mr. Ionescu, this is [Name] from [Property]. We're looking forward to having you back on Saturday! I've reserved your favorite room [number]. Do you need anything special this time?"
  - Review their guest profile for preferences: last room, dietary needs, favorite wine, any complaints from previous stays (address proactively — "Last time we had an issue with the hot water, which has been fully resolved").
- **For long-stay guests** (5+ nights): call them 5 days before arrival. "We wanted to check if there's anything we can prepare for your extended stay — extra pillows, a specific room temperature, any dietary needs we should know about?"
- **Internal pre-arrival action**: housekeeping prepares VIP rooms per the guest profile (welcome amenity, specific room temperature, preferred pillow type, a personal note from the owner). Front desk prepares the arrivals list with pre-calculated upsell opportunities per HM-008.

### Pas 3: Phase 3 — Arrival and Check-in (the first impression)
- See HM-001 (Front Office Daily Ops) for the full operational check-in procedure. The lifecycle additions focus on emotional experience, not operations:
- **Recognition**: greet by name from the moment identification is possible. For guests who provided a photo (some OTAs include profile photos), the front desk agent can recognize them before they reach the desk.
  - First-time guests: "Welcome to [Property], Mr. Ionescu — we've been expecting you!" (Enthusiastic, welcoming.)
  - Repeat guests: "Welcome back, Mrs. Popescu — it's wonderful to see you again! We have your usual room ready." (Recognition, warmth, continuity.)
- **Personalized orientation**: the front desk agent serves as the guest's host, not a process operator.
  - Tailor the orientation to the guest segment: a business traveler wants to know about Wi-Fi speed and a quiet workspace. A foodie wants to know about tonight's menu and the cooking class. A family wants to know about the play area and child-friendly dining options.
  - Read the guest's energy: if they are tired from travel, provide the essentials (Wi-Fi, restaurant, room) and offer a detailed orientation later. If they are excited and engaged, take 2 extra minutes to share the property's story and hidden gems.
- **Preference collection**: use the check-in conversation to learn preferences for the guest profile.
  - "Do you have a preference for a high or low floor?"
  - "Any dietary needs we should know about for breakfast or dinner?"
  - "Is there a special occasion during your stay?"
  - Enter immediately into the PMS guest profile — this information drives personalization for this stay and all future stays.
- **Room escort** (for VIPs, first-time guests at premium rate, and whenever staffing allows): physically walk the guest to their room. Show them the room features — how to operate the AC, where the Wi-Fi password is, the view from the window, the bathroom amenities. Check: "Is everything to your liking? Is there anything else you need?"
- **Welcome amenity delivery timing**: for VIP rooms, the amenity should be in the room before the guest arrives. If timed for after check-in (e.g., a surprise during turndown), the housekeeping team must coordinate with front desk.

### Pas 4: Phase 4 — During Stay (the experience window)
- **Mid-stay touchpoint** (for stays of 2+ nights — the highest-ROI action in guest lifecycle management):
  - On the second morning, either at the breakfast table or via a brief phone call: "Good morning, Mr. Ionescu! I wanted to check in — how is everything so far? Is there anything we can do to make your stay even better?"
  - This single question catches 80% of issues before they become departure complaints or negative reviews. A guest who is asked "how is everything?" and can voice a minor concern (pillow too soft, room too cold, noisy neighbors) while it can still be fixed will not complain at checkout or in a review. A guest who is never asked will stew silently and express it online.
  - If the guest identifies an issue: resolve immediately per HM-009. Follow up the same day.
  - If the guest is happy: reinforce it. "That's wonderful — we're so glad you're enjoying it. Don't forget about tonight's dinner — our chef has something special planned."
- **Proactive experience suggestions**: the front desk and F&B teams should proactively suggest activities and experiences throughout the stay, not just at check-in.
  - "Have you walked the forest trail yet? The morning light is beautiful around 08:00."
  - "Tonight our chef is preparing a special tasting menu — shall I reserve your table?"
  - "Tomorrow is forecast to be sunny — would you like to have breakfast on the terrace?"
  - These suggestions should feel natural and helpful, not scripted. Train staff to observe guest behavior and match suggestions to visible interests.
- **Surprise and delight for special occasions**: if the reservation notes a birthday, anniversary, honeymoon, or any celebration:
  - Coordinate a surprise: birthday cake in the room, anniversary card signed by the team, a complimentary glass of champagne at dinner, rose petals on the bed during turndown, a small local gift (handmade craft, local honey, artisan chocolate).
  - Cost: 30–80 RON per surprise. Return: these moments generate the most powerful positive reviews, the most Instagram-worthy photos, and the strongest emotional loyalty. The ROI is extraordinary.
  - Timing: deliver the surprise at a moment the guest does not expect. The turndown service is ideal for room surprises. At dinner, bring the cake after dessert when the mood is already positive.
- **Activity and local engagement**: for a rural property, the surroundings are part of the product.
  - Provide a welcome pack with: property map, local walking trails (printed with distances and difficulty), nearby attractions with brief descriptions and driving times, local restaurant recommendations (if your property's restaurant is not open for every meal), emergency contacts.
  - Offer equipment: walking sticks, binoculars for birdwatching, picnic baskets (for purchase/rental), bicycles (if available).
  - Partner with local experience providers: guided nature walks, wine cellar visits, pottery workshops, farm visits. Earn a referral commission (10%) while enriching the guest experience.

### Pas 5: Phase 5 — Check-Out (the lasting impression)
- See HM-001 for the full operational checkout procedure. Lifecycle additions:
- **Verbal satisfaction check**: when presenting the folio, ask genuinely: "How was your overall stay?" Listen actively. Do not defend shortcomings. If the response is positive, plant the review seed: "That's wonderful to hear — if you have a moment, a review on Google or Booking.com would mean a lot to us as a small property."
- **If there is a complaint at checkout**: follow HM-009 immediately. Never let a guest leave with unresolved dissatisfaction — the period between checkout frustration and review submission is typically 24–72 hours. An unresolved departure complaint almost certainly becomes a negative review.
- **Direct booking conversion**: this is the final in-person opportunity to convert OTA guests to direct.
  - "We'd love to have you back. If you book directly with us next time — by phone or our website — you'll receive [specific benefit: free breakfast, 10% F&B credit, late checkout, room upgrade on availability]."
  - Hand them a "Book Direct" card with the website URL, phone number, QR code, and benefits listed.
- **Email capture** (if not already in the system): "May I keep your email to send you occasional exclusive offers? We don't spam — just seasonal updates and special packages, 3–4 times per year."
  - GDPR compliance: record the consent with date and the specific purpose ("marketing communications").
  - Target: capture email from 60%+ of checked-in guests. Every email is a future direct booking opportunity.
- **Farewell**: the last 30 seconds of the stay are disproportionately influential on the guest's memory.
  - Walk the guest to the door or car if the setting allows naturally. "Thank you for choosing us, Mr. Ionescu — it was a genuine pleasure having you. Safe travels, and we hope to see you again soon."
  - For departing guests with children: a small parting gift for the child (a coloring book with the property's logo, a locally made sweet) creates a memory anchor for the entire family.

### Pas 6: Phase 6 — Post-Departure (24 hours to 12 months after checkout)
- **24-hour post-stay email** (automated via PMS or email tool):
  - Thank the guest by name for their stay. Reference something specific if possible ("We hope the fire-kitchen tasting menu was memorable!").
  - **Review request**: "If you enjoyed your stay, we'd be incredibly grateful for a quick review on [platform]. It means the world to a family-run property like ours." Include a direct link to the review page (Google My Business review link or Booking.com review page).
  - **Next stay offer**: "As a returning guest, your next direct booking includes [specific benefit]. Book anytime at [website URL] or call us at [number]."
  - "We hope to welcome you again soon."
  - Conversion benchmark: 10–15% of guests who receive a post-stay email leave a review. 3–5% make a return booking within 12 months directly from the email.
- **For high-value guests** (long-stay, corporate, VIP, recovery guests): send a personal email from the GM/owner within 48 hours, not an automated one. "Dear Mr. Ionescu, I wanted to personally thank you for choosing to stay with us. It was a pleasure hosting you, and I hope every aspect of your visit met your expectations. Please don't hesitate to contact me directly for your next visit — I'll personally ensure everything is arranged." Sign with the GM's name, direct email, and phone number.
- **Review monitoring** (ongoing): check all platforms within 48 hours of the expected review window. Respond per HM-009 protocol — all reviews within 48 hours, positive and negative.
- **90-day re-engagement email**: "It's been 3 months since your visit — we wanted to share what's new at [Property]." Include: new menu items, upcoming events, seasonal experiences, and a booking link with a returning-guest incentive.
- **12-month anniversary email**: "It's been exactly one year since your stay with us. We've been thinking about you and we'd love to welcome you back. As an anniversary gesture, here's [exclusive offer — 15% off, free dinner, complimentary upgrade] for any booking in the next 3 months."
- **Seasonal newsletter** (quarterly): sent to all past guests who opted in. Content: seasonal highlights at the property, new menu announcements, upcoming events, exclusive direct booking offers. Keep it brief (500 words maximum), visual (1–2 hero photos), and personal (written in the owner's voice, not marketing copy). Unsubscribe rate below 2% per send indicates healthy content quality.
- **Guest database segmentation for communication** (see HM-006):
  - Segment the email list by: guest segment, stay recency, spend level, occasion type, and channel.
  - Tailor communication: foodie guests receive menu update emails. Corporate guests receive retreat package emails. Local guests receive weekend escape promotions. Retreat guests receive next-season program announcements.
  - Personalization increases open rates by 30–50% and click-through rates by 50–100% compared to generic blasts.

### Pas 7: Loyalty Program Design (Optional — for properties with 500+ guests per year)
- A formal loyalty program may be justified when the guest volume supports it. For smaller properties, informal loyalty (personal recognition, returning-guest benefits, direct booking perks) is sufficient and often more impactful.
- **Simple loyalty structure for independent properties**:
  - After 1st stay: automatic "Returning Guest" status with a benefit (free welcome drink on next visit).
  - After 3rd stay: "Valued Guest" status with enhanced benefits (guaranteed room upgrade on availability, priority reservation at restaurant, 10% F&B discount).
  - After 5th stay: "VIP Guest" status with premium benefits (complimentary upgrade always, birthday surprise, annual invitation to a private event, direct contact with owner/GM).
- Track loyalty status in the PMS guest profile. Ensure front desk agents can see the status at check-in and acknowledge it.
- **Cost of loyalty program**: the benefits cost 2–5% of room revenue for loyalty guests. The return: loyalty guests have near-zero acquisition cost, 15–25% higher average spend, and 5x higher lifetime value than one-time guests. The math overwhelmingly favors investment in loyalty.

### Pas 8: Guest Data Strategy — Owning the Relationship
- Every guest who consents to communication is an owned marketing asset. Unlike OTA traffic (which you rent at 15–18% commission per booking), your email list has zero ongoing cost.
- **Data collection touchpoints**: booking confirmation (email), check-in (phone, preferences, dietary needs, occasion), during stay (activity preferences, F&B preferences), checkout (feedback, GDPR consent), post-stay (review platform engagement).
- **Data storage**: all guest data lives in the PMS guest profile. For email marketing, sync PMS profiles to your email tool (Mailchimp/Brevo). Most PMS systems support integration or CSV export for this purpose.
- **Data privacy**: comply fully with GDPR. Maintain records of consent. Provide easy unsubscribe. Respond to data access or deletion requests within 30 days. Never sell or share guest data with third parties.
- **Long-term goal**: build a guest database of 2,000+ opted-in contacts within 3 years. At a 3% booking conversion rate, this database generates 60+ direct bookings per year — worth 15,000–30,000 RON in saved OTA commissions alone.

## Note
- **The mid-stay touchpoint is the highest-ROI action in this entire procedure.** If you implement only one thing from this lifecycle framework, implement the second-morning check-in question. It prevents negative reviews, surfaces fixable problems, and makes guests feel genuinely cared for.
- **Common mistake — over-automating the lifecycle**: automation is essential for consistency (confirmation emails, post-stay emails), but the most impactful touchpoints are personal (owner's WhatsApp, mid-stay check-in, farewell at checkout). A property that automates everything and personalizes nothing feels like a machine. A property that personalizes the key moments and automates the routine feels like a warm host with good systems.
- **Edge case — guest who opted out of communication**: respect the opt-out completely. No emails, no messages, no calls except for operational necessities (check-in time, checkout reminder). Note the preference in PMS. These guests still deserve exceptional in-person service — they just do not want digital follow-up.
- **Albastru / Sapte Focuri context**: the personal nature of a boutique property is a competitive advantage at every touchpoint. Where a chain hotel sends automated emails from "reservations@chain.com," a WhatsApp from the property owner the day before arrival creates a fundamentally different emotional response. Use personal communication where automation would feel generic. The owner's personal involvement in the guest lifecycle — from the WhatsApp before arrival to the farewell at checkout — is the most powerful loyalty mechanism available. It is also the most authentic expression of what makes the property special. The fire-kitchen experience should be woven into every lifecycle phase: the confirmation email mentions it, the pre-arrival email offers to book a table, the mid-stay check asks if they have tried it yet, the post-stay email references the dining experience. The kitchen is not just an amenity — it is the protagonist of the guest story.

# Housekeeping Quality Standards Checklist

## Obiectiv
Define a repeatable, inspectable standard for room cleaning, turndown, and public area maintenance so that every guest room meets the same quality level regardless of which attendant services it — targeting a minimum 8.5/10 inspection average, zero guest complaints about cleanliness within the first 2 hours of check-in, and a room turnaround time under 45 minutes from checkout to "clean and inspected."

## Pași

### Pas 1: Briefing and Room Assignment (07:00 each morning)
- Housekeeping supervisor prints the Room Status Report from PMS: V/D (Vacant Dirty — priority 1), O/D (Occupied Dirty — stay-over rooms), V/C (Vacant Clean — inspect only), OOO (skip, note reason).
- Assign rooms to attendants by section. Industry standard: 12–16 rooms per attendant per 8-hour shift for a mid-scale property. Boutique properties with larger rooms, premium amenities, or detailed turndown service should budget 10–12 rooms per shift. Exceeding the assignment load leads to shortcuts — the number one cause of quality failures.
- Priority order for cleaning: (1) Checked-out rooms where a same-day arrival is expected (revenue-generating — these fund the payroll), (2) Checked-out rooms with no same-day arrival, (3) Stay-over rooms with "Clean Now" requests or DND removal, (4) Stay-over rooms by floor order.
- Brief the team on: VIP rooms requiring special setup (welcome amenities, extra pillows, specific room temperature), late checkouts that will delay room availability, group arrivals with a rooming list (bulk room prep needed), maintenance lock-outs, and any allergy or accessibility requirements (hypoallergenic linen, extra towel bars at wheelchair height).
- Distribute trolley supplies per the par stock checklist: 3 sets of linen per room type on trolley, amenity kits (shampoo, conditioner, shower gel, soap, dental kit, sewing kit), cleaning chemicals (all-purpose cleaner, glass cleaner, bathroom disinfectant, toilet bowl cleaner, descaler), color-coded cloths, trash bags, guest stationery, and the inspection clipboard.
- Safety reminder: proper lifting technique for mattresses, wet floor signage for corridors, chemical handling gloves required for all bathroom cleaning. Report any slip hazards, broken glass, or biohazard situations immediately — never attempt to clean biohazard without proper PPE and training.

### Pas 2: Enter Room Protocol
- Knock three times, firmly, and announce "Housekeeping" in a clear voice. Wait 10 seconds. Knock again and announce. Wait 10 seconds. If no response, use the master key to enter slowly while announcing again.
- If the DND (Do Not Disturb) card is on the door: log the room number and time, skip, return after 2 hours. If DND has been displayed continuously for more than 24 hours, notify the supervisor — a welfare check may be warranted (property policy should define the threshold, typically 24–36 hours).
- If a guest is in the room unexpectedly: "I'm sorry to disturb you — shall I come back later? What time would be convenient?" Never enter a room with a guest present without explicit permission.
- Open the door fully, prop with the trolley to signal the room is being serviced by staff (security measure — prevents unauthorized entry while the room is open).
- First assessment upon entry: (1) Safety scan — no suspicious items, packages, or unauthorized guests. If anything unusual, do NOT touch, exit the room, lock the door, notify the supervisor immediately. (2) Lost property check — any items left by previous guests go into a labeled Lost & Found bag with room number, date, and description. Log in the Lost & Found Register. (3) Damage assessment — any new damage to furniture, walls, fixtures, or linens is noted on the Room Condition Report before cleaning begins.
- Open curtains and windows (weather permitting) to air the room. Turn on all lights for full visibility — you cannot clean what you cannot see. Check that all lights work during this step.

### Pas 3: Bed Making Standard
- Strip all linen from the bed onto the floor or directly into the linen bag. Never shake linen in the room — it disperses dust, allergens, and microscopic contaminants.
- Inspect the mattress: check for stains (mark for deep cleaning or mattress protector replacement), damage, and pest signs (bed bugs leave small dark spots and shed skins along seams — check seams, headboard joints, and nightstand crevices). If any pest signs are found, stop immediately, do NOT make the bed, seal the room, and report to the supervisor. The room goes OOO until professional pest treatment is completed and verified.
- Mattress protector: verify it is clean and positioned correctly. Replace if stained or if the previous guest stayed more than 7 nights.
- Fitted sheet: secure all four corners with hospital corners. Sheet must be smooth with no wrinkles — run your hand across the entire surface. A wrinkled sheet is the most visible quality failure in housekeeping.
- Top sheet: fold back 30 cm at the head of the bed, aligned symmetrically with the mattress edges. The fold should be crisp and even on both sides.
- Duvet or blanket: center on the bed with equal overhang on both sides. For duvet covers, ensure the duvet insert is shaken to fill all corners evenly — a lumpy duvet signals lazy preparation.
- Pillows: fluff and position upright against the headboard. Pillowcases with the opening side facing away from the room entrance (the side guests see first when they walk in). Standard: 2 pillows per sleeping position. If the property offers a pillow menu (soft/firm/hypoallergenic), check the guest profile for preferences on stay-over cleans.
- Decorative cushions/throws: arrange per the property standard photo (keep a laminated reference photo in the supervisor's folder and on each trolley). Consistency across rooms is critical — a guest who sees their friend's room arranged differently feels short-changed.
- Bed making time benchmark: 8–10 minutes for a double/king bed including full linen change. Timed training during onboarding ensures speed without quality loss.

### Pas 4: Bathroom Cleaning Sequence (dirty-to-clean order)
The bathroom is the most scrutinized area by guests. A single hair on the floor negates every other effort.
1. **Spray and dwell**: spray toilet bowl interior with disinfectant. Let it dwell (minimum 5 minutes contact time) while cleaning the rest of the bathroom. This dwell time is non-negotiable for proper disinfection.
2. **Mirrors and glass**: clean with glass cleaner and a lint-free cloth. Check from multiple angles — a streak visible in certain light is visible to the guest. Polish until zero streaks.
3. **Vanity and sink**: wipe the vanity surface, clean the sink bowl, polish the faucet. Dry all chrome fixtures to prevent water spots. Check under the sink for any debris or standing water (leak indicator).
4. **Shower/bath**: scrub all surfaces — tiles, glass door/curtain, bath floor, shower tray. Remove soap residue and lime scale from chrome fixtures. Clean the drain cover — hair in the drain is a hygiene failure. For glass shower doors: squeegee after cleaning, then dry-polish.
5. **Toilet**: scrub inside the bowl (under the rim is the most neglected area), clean the seat (top, bottom, and hinges), wipe the outside of the bowl, clean the base and floor around the toilet. Use a dedicated color-coded cloth — the toilet cloth (typically red) is NEVER used on any other surface.
6. **Floor**: mop the entire bathroom floor, including behind the toilet and under the vanity overhang. Use a fresh mop head for each room (or hot-wash between rooms if using reusable mops). Work from the far corner toward the door.
7. **Towels**: replace all towels per the property standard. Folding standard: bath towel tri-folded on the towel rack, hand towel folded and centered on the vanity or rack, face cloth folded into a triangle or rolled. For stay-over rooms participating in the linen reuse program: replace only towels left on the floor per the reuse card instructions.
8. **Amenities**: replenish to par level. Refillable dispensers: verify dispensers are at least 50% full (top up from bulk stock). Individual amenities: replace used items, position consistently. Toilet paper: fold the first sheet into a point or triangle (the "housekeeping has been here" signal). Spare roll placed visibly in the toilet roll holder or on the vanity.
9. **Final inspection**: step back and scan the entire bathroom. Check: no hair anywhere (floor, sink, shower, toilet), no water on the floor, no product residue on surfaces, bin emptied and re-lined, pleasant scent (a single spray of room freshener if property standard permits — never overwhelming), all amenities aligned. The 30-second final scan is where 80% of recleans are prevented.

### Pas 5: Room Cleaning — Bedroom and Living Areas
- Dust all surfaces in a systematic top-to-bottom sweep: ceiling corners (cobwebs), light fittings and lampshades, top of wardrobe and shelves, picture frames, window sills, headboard, bedside tables, desk surface, chair legs, skirting boards. Use a damp microfiber cloth — dry dusting redistributes dust.
- Clean windows and glass surfaces (balcony door, mirrors). Check for fingerprints on glass surfaces at guest-touching height.
- Wardrobe and drawers: open all, check for items left by previous guests, wipe interior surfaces, verify hangers are at par (6–10 hangers per room with trouser clips on at least 2), laundry bag and price list are present, safe is open and empty (reset the code if electronic), iron and ironing board present if property standard.
- Empty all bins. Re-line with fresh bags. Check under the desk and behind the nightstand for dropped trash.
- Minibar check: record consumed items on the minibar consumption slip with room number, date, and items consumed. Place the slip in the designated tray for front office posting. Restock consumed items to par. Check expiration dates on all items monthly — expired stock is a quality failure and potential liability.
- Electronics check: turn on the TV (correct channel/welcome screen), verify AC remote functions and is set to property default (22–23 degrees C), test both bedside lamps, check that all power sockets are functional, verify the clock/alarm is set to the correct time, confirm the telephone works (dial front desk from the room).
- Wi-Fi signal: if the property has a signal strength concern, test the Wi-Fi in each room periodically (weekly rotation) and report dead zones to maintenance/IT.
- Vacuum carpet or mop hard floor — this is always the final cleaning step. Work from the far corner toward the door. Use an edge nozzle along baseboards. For hard floors, check for scuff marks (use a melamine sponge for spot removal).
- Replenish stationery, guest directory/compendium, TV guide, room service menu, and any property-specific items (walking trail maps, restaurant menu, local guide). Align all items parallel to the desk edge.
- Scent: the room should smell clean and neutral. If there is a persistent odor (smoke, food, dampness), do not mask with air freshener — report to the supervisor for deep odor treatment (ozone machine, fabric sanitizer).
- Final room walkthrough: stand at the door and view the room as the guest will see it upon entry. Is the bed inviting? Are surfaces clear and organized? Is the lighting warm? Are curtains evenly drawn? The first 3 seconds of visual impression determine the guest's quality perception.

### Pas 6: Supervisor Inspection
- Supervisor inspects a minimum of 30% of rooms cleaned daily (industry best practice upgraded from 20%) and 100% of VIP arrival rooms and rooms that received a complaint during the previous stay.
- Use the Room Inspection Scorecard with detailed scoring across 6 zones:
  - **Bed** (0–10): linen crispness, symmetry, pillow arrangement, no wrinkles, decorative items in place.
  - **Bathroom** (0–10): cleanliness of all surfaces, chrome polish, amenity placement, no hair/residue, towel folding standard, toilet bowl interior spotless.
  - **Floor** (0–10): carpet/hard floor clean, no debris, edges cleaned, under-bed visible area clear.
  - **Surfaces and furniture** (0–10): dust-free on all tested surfaces (supervisor runs a white-gloved finger along 3 random surfaces), glass streak-free, wardrobe interior clean, desk organized.
  - **Amenities and supplies** (0–10): all amenities at par, stationery complete, minibar stocked, correct items per room type, no expired items.
  - **Smell and ambience** (0–10): fresh neutral scent, correct lighting setup, temperature comfortable, curtains correctly positioned, visual impression from doorway.
- Minimum pass score: 8.0/10 average. Rooms scoring below 8.0 are returned to the attendant for re-clean before being released for guest occupancy. The attendant is told exactly which items failed — never a vague "clean it again."
- Record all scores in the daily Quality Log. Track each attendant's rolling 4-week average score. Attendants consistently scoring above 9.0 should be recognized (bonus, employee of the month). Attendants consistently below 8.0 after coaching require a performance improvement discussion (see HM-015).
- Rooms that pass inspection: supervisor changes PMS status to "Vacant Clean" (VC) or "Occupied Clean" (OC). Only the supervisor should change a room status to VC — never the attendant who cleaned it (separation of duties for quality control).

### Pas 7: Turndown Service (if offered)
- Turndown is typically offered between 17:00 and 20:00 for properties positioned at mid-scale or above. For boutique properties, it is a powerful luxury signal even with a simple execution.
- Turndown checklist:
  - Close curtains/blinds.
  - Turn down the bed: fold the duvet back diagonally on the side closest to the nightstand, place a goodnight amenity on the pillow (chocolate, local sweet, mint).
  - Place a bedside water bottle and glass on the nightstand.
  - Adjust lighting to low/ambient (bedside lamp on, ceiling light off).
  - Replace any used towels or glasses from earlier in the day.
  - Empty bins.
  - Straighten any displaced items.
  - Place slippers at the bedside (if amenity standard includes slippers).
  - Place the next day's weather card or a "Good Morning" card with breakfast hours on the nightstand.
- Turndown time per room: 5–8 minutes. A single attendant can turn down 20–25 rooms in a 3-hour window.

### Pas 8: Public Area Cleaning Schedule
- Public areas (lobby, corridors, stairs, elevators, restaurant, reception, guest bathrooms) require a continuous cleaning schedule — they are visible to every guest all day.
- **Morning deep clean** (06:00–07:00 before guests are active): vacuum/mop all corridors and lobby, clean all glass surfaces, polish elevator interiors, clean guest bathrooms thoroughly, empty all public bins, wipe down all furniture.
- **Midday touchup** (12:00–13:00): spot-check corridors and lobby, clean guest bathrooms again (high-traffic period), restock bathroom amenities.
- **Afternoon touchup** (16:00–17:00): pre-dinner cleaning of restaurant and bar area, corridor and lobby spot-check, guest bathroom refresh.
- **Evening check** (21:00): final guest bathroom check, lobby tidy, corridor walkthrough.
- After events or high-traffic periods (breakfast clear-down, group arrivals, restaurant service): immediate cleaning response. Never leave a public area visibly dirty during guest operating hours.

### Pas 9: Linen and Laundry Control
- Soiled linen is bagged separately: standard soiled (normal wash cycle) vs. heavily stained (pre-soak/special treatment required). Use dedicated, labeled laundry bags — mixing categories wastes time at the laundry facility.
- Count linen pieces sent to laundry against the Linen Control Sheet. Count linen returned. Reconcile daily. Persistent shortfalls indicate loss (misrouted, stolen, or damaged beyond use).
- Par stock per room: industry standard is 3 complete sets (one in room, one in laundry, one on shelf/trolley ready to deploy). For properties with in-house laundry: 2.5 sets may suffice. For properties using external laundry with 48-hour turnaround: 3.5 sets required.
- Monthly linen audit: physical count of all linen in circulation (rooms + laundry + storage). Compare against opening inventory. Calculate loss rate. Acceptable annual linen loss: 10–15% (wear and tear, staining, misrouting). Above 15% indicates a control problem requiring investigation.
- Replacement budgeting: budget 8–12% of housekeeping operating cost for annual linen replacement. A fraying towel or yellowed sheet immediately reduces perceived room quality — replace proactively, not reactively.
- Linen reuse program (see HM-019 Eco-Friendly Operations): for stays of 2+ nights, place a linen reuse card explaining the environmental benefit. Towels left on the hook = reuse; towels on the floor = replace. Industry participation rate: 60–70% of guests. Cost saving: 15–20% reduction in laundry volume and chemical use.

### Pas 10: Lost and Found Procedure
- Any guest item found during room cleaning is immediately placed in a clear, labeled bag: room number, date found, attendant name, item description.
- Valuable items (electronics, jewelry, documents, cash): delivered immediately to the supervisor, who logs in the Lost & Found Register and places in the hotel safe. Contact the guest same-day via the contact details in PMS.
- Non-valuable items: logged in the register, stored in the Lost & Found room. Retain for a minimum of 90 days (check local legal requirements — Romanian civil code has specific lost property obligations).
- If a guest calls about a lost item: search the register first, then check the room if it has not been cleaned since checkout. If found, arrange shipping at the guest's expense (with their credit card authorization). This is a loyalty-building touchpoint — a property that proactively returns a forgotten item creates a guest for life.

## Verificare
- [ ] All assigned rooms cleaned and status updated in PMS before 15:00 (checkout rooms before 13:00 if same-day arrivals expected)
- [ ] Supervisor inspection scorecard completed for minimum 30% of rooms (100% for VIP/arrival rooms)
- [ ] Average inspection score above 8.0/10 (team aggregate)
- [ ] Maintenance faults logged on Maintenance Request Form — zero deferred items older than 48 hours
- [ ] Minibar consumption slips handed to front office by 15:00 daily
- [ ] Linen control sheet balanced for the day — send/return count matched
- [ ] No guest complaints related to cleanliness received within 2 hours of check-in (zero tolerance target)
- [ ] Public areas cleaned per the published schedule — spot-check log signed by supervisor
- [ ] Lost & Found Register current — all items logged within 1 hour of discovery

## Instrumente
- PMS room status module (or laminated room status board with dry-erase markers for small properties without PMS)
- Housekeeping trolley with par stock checklist (laminated, attached to trolley)
- Room Inspection Scorecard (printable template — one per room per inspection)
- Linen Control Sheet (daily send/receive log)
- Color-coded cleaning cloths: red = toilet, blue = bathroom surfaces, yellow = bedroom surfaces, green = glass. Never cross-contaminate.
- Maintenance Request Form (carbon copy — one copy stays with housekeeping, one goes to maintenance)
- Lost & Found Register (bound logbook or shared digital spreadsheet)
- Chemical safety data sheets (SDS) posted in the housekeeping storage area per HSE requirements
- Cleaning chemical dilution chart posted at the housekeeping sink — correct dilution prevents surface damage and chemical waste

## Note
- **Never use a guest's towel or linen to clean surfaces** — this is both a hygiene violation and a liability issue. Even if the guest has checked out, the towels may be reused for the next guest if they appear unused.
- **Common mistake — speed over quality**: an attendant rushing through 18 rooms will consistently score below 7/10. Twelve well-cleaned rooms are more valuable than eighteen poorly cleaned rooms — one bad room can generate a negative review that costs thousands in lost bookings.
- **Common mistake — the under-bed blind spot**: guests drop items and dust accumulates under beds. Include a flashlight check under the bed in every departure clean.
- **Edge case — guest refuses housekeeping for multiple days**: respect the DND, but after 3 consecutive days, the supervisor should offer a "light refresh" (towels and trash only) for hygiene reasons. If refused, log it and have the Duty Manager do a security/welfare check.
- **Edge case — blood or biohazard in room**: do NOT attempt normal cleaning. Seal the room. Notify the supervisor and Duty Manager. Use PPE (gloves, apron, face shield) and a biohazard cleaning kit. Follow blood-borne pathogen protocol. Document the incident. The room may need professional deep cleaning before returning to service.
- **Albastru / Șapte Focuri context**: for a boutique rural property, the housekeeping team is small (likely 2–4 attendants for 6–10 rooms). This is an advantage — consistent quality is easier to maintain with a small, well-trained team. The natural setting introduces unique cleaning challenges: pollen in spring/summer (clean window sills and air filters more frequently), insects in summer (screen windows, provide plug-in repellent in rooms), mud in autumn/winter (provide a boot cleaning station at the entrance, use a heavy-duty doormat in the lobby). The property's positioning as a nature retreat means guests expect a visually beautiful, naturally scented environment — avoid chemical air fresheners; let fresh air and natural scents (lavender sachets, dried herbs in a ceramic dish) define the room ambience. If using wood-burning heating, train housekeeping on fireplace/stove ash management and ensure fire safety checklists include checking that the fire is fully extinguished before leaving the room.

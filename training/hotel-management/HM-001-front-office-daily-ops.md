# Front Office Daily Operations SOP

## Obiectiv
Standardize all front office activities across three shifts (morning, afternoon, night) to ensure seamless guest check-in/check-out, accurate room status, zero revenue leakage, and a measurable upsell conversion rate of at least 15% — treating the front desk as both a service hub and a revenue center.

## Pași

### Pas 1: Shift Handover Protocol (first 15 minutes of each shift)
- Read the Night/Previous Shift Log cover to cover: outstanding issues, VIP arrivals, maintenance flags, special requests, group movements, expected late check-ins, and any guest whose name the incoming agent should know.
- Verify the cash float against the float sheet. Count every denomination — do not estimate. Discrepancies must be resolved and documented before the outgoing agent leaves. Industry standard float: 500–1,500 RON depending on property size.
- Review the arrivals list for the next 4 hours. For each arrival, flag: early check-in requests (coordinate with housekeeping by 08:00), group blocks with rooming lists pending, room type upgrade opportunities, prepaid vs. pay-on-arrival status, and any OTA special instructions (e.g., Genius benefits on Booking.com).
- Check the PMS dashboard for room inventory health: rooms out-of-order (OOO) with estimated return date, out-of-service (OOS), complimentary and house-use rooms, and any overbooking alerts. Calculate available sellable rooms = total inventory minus OOO minus confirmed bookings minus buffer for walk-ins (typically 1–2 rooms).
- Sync with the housekeeping supervisor on the ready-room count vs. expected arrivals. If the gap is tight (arrivals > clean rooms by more than 2), agree on a priority cleaning order by 08:30. Communicate early check-in promises only after housekeeping confirms capacity.
- Verify communication equipment: phone system operational, key card encoder tested (encode a test card), POS terminal charged and connected, guest Wi-Fi working (run a speed test — guests expect minimum 10 Mbps download).
- Check weather forecast and local event calendar for the day — front desk agents should proactively inform guests about relevant conditions ("There's a light rain expected this afternoon — would you like an umbrella for your walk?").

### Pas 2: Morning Arrival Processing (06:00–14:00)
- Pull the arrivals list from PMS and sort by priority: (1) VIPs and repeat guests, (2) loyalty members or guests with special occasions noted, (3) early check-in requests, (4) group arrivals with coordinator, (5) standard reservations.
- Pre-assign rooms using the ABCD rule:
  - **A**ssign: match exactly to the booked room type. Never downgrade without pre-communication and compensation.
  - **B**lock: flag upgrade-eligible rooms for VIPs, loyalty members, and upsell candidates (identify the price delta before the guest arrives).
  - **C**heck: verify bed configuration, floor preference, connecting room needs for families, and accessibility requirements.
  - **D**istribute: balance assignments across floors to spread housekeeping workload and avoid noise clustering (do not assign a family with children next to a couple celebrating an anniversary).
- Prepare registration cards or digital check-in QR links for all same-day arrivals. For digital check-in, the confirmation must still be verified at the desk — digital check-in is a time-saver, not a security bypass.
- Place welcome amenity orders with housekeeping for VIP, honeymoon, birthday, and anniversary rooms before 09:00. Standard welcome amenity cost budget: 30–80 RON per VIP room (bottle of local wine, seasonal fruit plate, handwritten card from the GM). Track amenity cost per VIP arrival monthly.
- Action upsell opportunity flags: for each standard room arrival where a superior room is available, note the upsell price delta. Pre-calculate and write it on the arrivals list so the agent does not need to look it up during the guest interaction. Target: attempt upsell on 100% of eligible check-ins.
- For group arrivals: confirm rooming list is finalized and room block is assigned. Prepare group registration packet (all keys pre-encoded, group welcome letter, event schedule if MICE). Designate one agent as the group coordinator for the entire check-in process.

### Pas 3: Check-In Execution
- Greet the guest within 30 seconds of approaching the desk. If there is a queue, make eye contact and acknowledge: "Good afternoon — I'll be with you in just a moment." Never let a guest stand unacknowledged.
- Use the guest's name from the moment you identify them. "Welcome, Mr. Ionescu — we've been expecting you." For repeat guests: "Welcome back, Mrs. Popescu — it's wonderful to see you again."
- Verify identity (passport or ID card — Romanian law requires registration of all guests). Scan or photocopy the ID document. For international guests, confirm visa status if applicable. Record nationality in PMS for tourism reporting.
- Confirm reservation details verbally: dates, room type, rate, inclusions (breakfast, parking, dinner package). This prevents post-stay disputes.
- Collect payment method: credit card pre-authorization for the estimated stay cost plus 20% incidentals buffer. If the card declines, handle discretely — never announce it aloud. "I'm having a small technical issue with the terminal — would you mind trying another card, or shall we try once more?"
- Execute the upsell script (see HM-008 for the full framework). Timing: after the reservation is confirmed but before issuing keys. "I noticed we actually have a Deluxe Room available tonight with a beautiful garden view — it's just 80 RON more. Would you like me to upgrade you?"
- Issue key cards (always two per room unless guest requests one). Write the room number on the key card sleeve — never say the room number aloud in the lobby (GDPR and security best practice).
- Provide a concise property orientation (60 seconds maximum): restaurant hours and reservation recommendation, breakfast time and location, Wi-Fi network name and password, emergency exit directions, and one highlight: "Our chef cooks everything over an open fire — I'd really recommend trying dinner tonight."
- Input all check-in data into PMS within 2 minutes of the guest leaving the desk. Change room status to "Occupied." If the system is slow, note on paper and enter within 15 minutes — never leave a status gap.
- Target check-in time from greeting to key handover: 5 minutes for returning guests, 8 minutes for new guests, 12 minutes for groups per individual.

### Pas 4: Check-Out Execution
- Print the folio 30 minutes before the expected departure time. Review it yourself before the guest sees it — look for: charges posted to the wrong room, duplicate F&B charges, missing minibar charges, unauthorized discounts, and any charge that might surprise the guest.
- Flag disputed or potentially confusing charges (e.g., minibar charges the guest may not remember, foreign currency exchange differences) to the supervisor before the guest arrives.
- Present the itemized folio to the guest. Walk through it: "Here's your room charge for two nights, breakfast was included, and here are your dinner charges from last evening. Does everything look correct?"
- If the guest disputes a charge: remain calm, apologize for any confusion, and resolve using the pre-approved discount authority (see HM-009 Compensation Matrix). Front desk agents can waive charges up to 100 RON without manager approval. Never argue with the guest — the cost of a disputed 50 RON minibar charge is negligible compared to the cost of a negative review.
- Process payment: void the pre-authorization, charge the actual amount, issue a printed or emailed receipt. For room charges billed to a company, verify the billing address and PO number match the corporate agreement.
- Ask for feedback: "How was your stay, Mr. Ionescu?" If positive, plant the review seed: "That's wonderful to hear — if you have a moment, a review on Google or Booking.com would mean a lot to us." If negative, escalate to Guest Relations immediately (see HM-009).
- Offer the direct booking incentive: "Next time you'd like to visit, if you book directly with us by phone or our website, we'll include a complimentary breakfast upgrade."
- Change room status to "Vacant Dirty" in PMS. Send housekeeping notification immediately — every minute of delay between checkout and housekeeping arrival is lost turnaround time. Industry benchmark: room turnaround time from checkout to clean-and-inspected should be under 45 minutes.
- Safe travels farewell: if natural, walk the guest to the door. "Thank you for staying with us — we hope to see you again soon." The last 30 seconds of the stay are disproportionately influential on the guest's memory.

### Pas 5: Cashiering and Revenue Accuracy
- Reconcile POS transactions against PMS folio charges every 2 hours during service periods. Every F&B charge that appears in POS must have a matching posting on a guest folio in PMS. Common errors: charge posted to wrong room number (transposition error, e.g., room 12 vs. room 21), charge not posted at all (server forgot to post to folio), charge posted twice.
- Void corrections require supervisor signature and a detailed note in the Void Register: date, time, original charge amount, reason for void, authorizing supervisor name. Track total void value daily — an abnormal void rate (>2% of revenue) signals either operational problems or potential fraud.
- Foreign currency: accept only at rates published by the front office manager that morning. Update the rate board by 08:00 using the BNR (National Bank of Romania) exchange rate with a 3–5% margin. Keep a record of all foreign currency transactions for accounting reconciliation.
- Credit card chargebacks: if a chargeback notification arrives, pull the original registration card, signed folio, and any supporting documentation (guest signed for the charge) immediately. Respond to the card issuer within the deadline (typically 14 days). Prevention: always get a chip+PIN or signature at check-in pre-authorization.
- At shift close: run the "Shift Audit" report in PMS. Balance cash, card, and voucher totals against system totals. Prepare the deposit envelope with all cash above the float amount. Hand to the safe with a witness signature. Log in the Cash Drop Register.
- Petty cash: maintain a small petty cash fund (200–500 RON) for minor operational expenses (taxi for a guest, emergency supply purchase). Every disbursement requires a receipt stapled to a Petty Cash Voucher. Reconcile petty cash weekly.

### Pas 6: Walk-in and Overbooking Management
- Walk-in guests (no reservation): check real-time availability. If rooms are available, offer the BAR (Best Available Rate) — never offer a discount to walk-ins during high-demand periods. Walk-ins during low demand can be offered a same-day special if occupancy is below 50%.
- Overbooking protocol: if the property is overbooked (more confirmed reservations than available rooms — this happens due to no-show buffer or system errors):
  1. Identify the guest to be "walked" (relocated): always the most recently booked, non-VIP, non-direct booking guest. Never walk a repeat guest or a direct booking.
  2. Arrange alternative accommodation at a comparable or superior property before the guest arrives. Pay for their first night at the alternative property, arrange transport, and provide a direct phone number for your GM.
  3. Offer a future stay incentive: one complimentary night at your property on their next visit.
  4. Log the overbooking incident: date, cause (no-show overestimate, double booking, OTA sync failure), guest impacted, resolution cost. Review with revenue management to adjust the overbooking policy.
- Overbooking is a deliberate revenue management tactic in larger hotels (industry standard: overbook by 5–10% of capacity to offset no-shows). For boutique properties with fewer than 15 rooms, the risk of walking a guest outweighs the revenue gain — overbook with extreme caution or not at all.

### Pas 7: End-of-Day Operations and Night Shift Protocol
- Run the Night Audit in PMS (see HM-011 for the full night audit procedure). The night audit posts room charges, taxes, and package inclusions automatically. Verify all postings completed before 23:59.
- Generate the Daily Business Report (DBR): occupancy %, ADR, RevPAR, total room revenue, total F&B revenue, total revenue, comparison to budget and same day last year. Email to GM and Revenue Manager by 07:00. Industry benchmark targets for boutique rural Romania: occupancy 55–70% annually, ADR 250–500 RON depending on season and positioning, RevPAR 140–350 RON.
- Update the arrivals/departures board for the next day. Print the arrivals list with pre-assigned rooms and special requests for the morning team.
- Log all unresolved issues in the Handover Book: maintenance requests logged overnight, pending payments, guest complaints in progress, expected very early check-ins, and any security or safety incidents.
- Night security walkthrough: at minimum once between 01:00 and 04:00, walk the property exterior, check all entry points locked, verify fire exits unobstructed, check pool area secure (if applicable), and log the walkthrough time in the Security Log.
- Late-night check-ins: maintain the same check-in standard regardless of hour. Never rush through check-in formalities because it is 02:00 — the guest is tired and appreciates a warm, efficient welcome even more at that hour.

### Pas 8: Communication and Coordination Hub
- The front desk is the communication hub for all departments. Establish clear escalation paths:
  - **Maintenance request**: log in PMS or Maintenance Request system, notify maintenance immediately if urgent (e.g., no hot water, AC failure), follow up within 1 hour, confirm resolution with guest.
  - **F&B reservation**: front desk can make restaurant reservations for guests. Maintain a shared reservation book or system with the F&B team.
  - **Housekeeping rush request**: if a guest needs their room cleaned immediately (early return, spill), call housekeeping supervisor directly — do not rely on PMS status change alone.
  - **Medical emergency**: call 112, notify the Duty Manager, provide first aid if trained, document the incident in the Incident Report.
- Internal communication log: maintain a running log (physical or digital) of all inter-department communications during the shift. This prevents the "I told them but they didn't do it" problem.
- Guest messaging: if using a guest messaging platform (WhatsApp Business, hotel app), respond within 5 minutes during operating hours. After hours, set an auto-reply with emergency contact number.

### Pas 9: End-of-Shift Quality Self-Check
- Before leaving the shift, each front desk agent completes a 2-minute self-check:
  - [ ] All check-ins processed and PMS updated
  - [ ] All check-outs processed, folios settled, rooms changed to VD
  - [ ] Cash balanced, shift audit run, deposit prepared
  - [ ] Handover Log updated with everything the next shift needs to know
  - [ ] Desk area clean, organized, and stocked (registration cards, key cards, pens, brochures)
  - [ ] Upsell Log completed for the shift (attempts, conversions, revenue)
  - [ ] No guest waiting or unresolved request left open
- Sign the shift log. A signed log creates accountability and a clear audit trail.

## Verificare
- [ ] Cash float balanced at shift start and close — zero unexplained variances
- [ ] All arrivals pre-assigned in PMS before expected arrival time (target: 100% by 12:00)
- [ ] Upsell attempt logged for every eligible check-in (target: 100% attempt rate, 15%+ conversion)
- [ ] Night Audit completed with zero posting errors
- [ ] Handover Log updated and signed by outgoing agent — no verbal-only handovers
- [ ] Daily Business Report sent to GM by 07:00
- [ ] Average check-in time under 8 minutes for individual guests
- [ ] All guest IDs scanned/copied and stored per legal requirement
- [ ] No overbooking walks in the past 30 days (for boutique properties)

## Note
- **Early check-in requests**: never promise a room before housekeeping confirms "clean and inspected" status. Offer to store luggage and provide a complimentary welcome beverage while the guest waits. If the wait exceeds 1 hour past standard check-in time, the room assignment process needs review.
- **GDPR compliance**: never repeat the guest's room number aloud at the desk — write it on the key sleeve. Do not leave guest registration cards visible on the desk. Shred discarded registration documents.
- **Common mistake — multitasking during check-in**: agents who answer the phone or process another task while checking in a guest create the single worst first impression. The check-in guest has 100% attention priority. Let the phone go to voicemail if necessary.
- **Common mistake — information overload**: dumping 3 minutes of property information on a tired guest who just wants their room key. Read the guest's energy level. If they seem eager to go to the room, give the essentials (Wi-Fi, breakfast, restaurant) and offer a full orientation later: "Take your time settling in — if you'd like more details about activities or the area, I'm here anytime."
- **Edge case — guest arrives but room is not ready**: (1) Apologize sincerely, (2) Offer luggage storage, (3) Invite to the restaurant for a complimentary beverage, (4) Provide a specific time when the room will be ready (confirm with housekeeping before giving the time), (5) Personally escort them to the room when ready.
- **Edge case — power outage during check-in**: maintain a manual check-in kit at the desk (paper registration cards, manual key override instructions, flashlight, printed arrivals list from the morning). Practice the manual fallback once per quarter.
- **Albastru / Șapte Focuri context**: adapt PMS workflow to the property management tool in use. If no PMS yet, use a structured Google Sheet with columns: Room Number | Status (Vacant Clean / Vacant Dirty / Occupied / OOO) | Guest Name | Check-in Date | Check-out Date | Rate | Channel | Balance | Notes. Transition to a cloud PMS (recommended: Cloudbeds or Little Hotelier) when occupancy regularly exceeds 60% — manual management above that level creates unacceptable error risk. For a rural boutique property with 6–10 rooms, the front desk may also serve as the concierge, restaurant host, and activity booking point — cross-training is essential. The personal touch of the owner greeting guests at check-in is one of the most powerful service differentiators available (see HM-018).

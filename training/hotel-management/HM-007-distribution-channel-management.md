# Distribution Channel Management (OTA, Direct, GDS)

## Obiectiv
Optimize the mix of booking channels to maximize net revenue (after commissions and acquisition costs), reduce OTA dependency from a typical 65–75% to below 50% within 24 months, and grow the direct booking percentage by 5+ percentage points year-over-year — treating channel management as a profit optimization discipline, not a passive listing exercise.

## Pași

### Pas 1: Audit and Map Your Current Channels
- List every active booking channel. For each channel, collect these data points for the last 12 months:
  - **Commission rate or fee structure**: Booking.com 15–18% (default 15%, Preferred Partner 17–18%), Airbnb 3% host fee (+ guest service fee paid separately), Expedia 15–25%, BookRetreats.com (varies, typically 15–20%), Google Hotel Ads (CPC or commission model 10–15%), direct website (card processing 1.5–2.5% only), phone/email (zero commission), walk-in (zero commission).
  - **Booking volume**: room nights delivered per channel.
  - **Gross ADR**: average rate per channel.
  - **Net ADR**: Gross ADR minus commission/fees. This is the real revenue per room night from each channel.
  - **Cancellation rate**: critical for OTA channels (Booking.com free cancellation can run 20–35% depending on your cancellation policy settings). Cancellations consume operational resources (folio management, inventory release) without generating revenue.
  - **Effective commission**: (Commission Paid + Cost of Cancelled Bookings) / Revenue from Materialized Bookings. This is always higher than the stated commission rate. Example: 15% commission on 100 bookings, but 25 cancel = effective commission on the 75 that materialize is 20%.
  - **Average lead time**: days between booking and arrival. Shorter lead time = less time for pricing optimization.
  - **Guest type/segment**: which segments book through which channels? OTA tends to be FIT; direct tends to be repeat guests and corporates.
  - **Review contribution**: some channels generate more reviews. Booking.com auto-sends review requests, generating valuable social proof.
- **Rank channels by Net Revenue Contribution** = Net ADR x Volume (materialized bookings only). This ranking determines where to invest effort.

### Pas 2: Calculate the True Cost of Each Channel
- OTA cost is NOT just the headline commission. The full cost includes:
  - Commission on materialized bookings.
  - Operational cost of managing cancellations (staff time, inventory churn).
  - Cost of rate parity obligations (cannot offer lower OTA rates on direct channel — limits your pricing flexibility).
  - Cost of OTA promotional programs (Genius discount, Preferred Partner premium commission, visibility boosters) — these improve ranking but increase cost.
  - Cost of dependency: if 70% of bookings come from one OTA, you are operationally dependent. Any algorithm change, policy change, or account suspension would be catastrophic.
- **Direct channel cost** includes:
  - Card processing fees (1.5–2.5% of transaction value).
  - Website hosting and booking engine subscription (50–200 EUR/month for a good booking engine like Cloudbeds, SiteMinder, or SimplerBooking).
  - Marketing spend to drive direct traffic: Google Ads, Google Hotel Ads, social media ads, email marketing tool, SEO investment.
  - Total direct channel cost is typically 8–15% of direct revenue — significantly lower than OTA commission, but only if you actively market the direct channel. A passive "brand.com" site with no marketing investment generates few bookings.
- **Calculate the crossover point**: at what direct booking volume does the investment in a booking engine + marketing equal the commission savings vs. OTA? For most small properties, the crossover is at 2–3 direct bookings per month. Below that, the OTA is cheaper per booking. Above that, every additional direct booking saves 12–15% vs. OTA.

### Pas 3: OTA Presence Optimization
- **Booking.com** (typically the dominant OTA for European independent hotels):
  - Profile completeness: minimum 25 high-quality photos (professional photography strongly recommended — ROI is immediate in click-through rate improvement). Show: exterior, each room type (from multiple angles), bathroom, restaurant, kitchen (the fire), common areas, views, activities.
  - Description: lead with differentiators (HM-018). First 2 sentences determine whether the guest reads further. "The only traditional fire-kitchen accommodation in the region — dinner cooked in clay pots over an open fire, in a restored mountain property surrounded by forest."
  - Facilities list: tick every applicable facility. Each ticked facility improves searchability and filtering match.
  - Genius program: enrolling gives you access to Genius-level travelers (frequent bookers with higher average spend). The cost is a 10% discount to Genius guests. Worth it for visibility — but monitor the volume of Genius bookings vs. standard. If Genius exceeds 40% of Booking.com bookings, the discount cost becomes significant.
  - Preferred Partner program: gives higher ranking in search results in exchange for higher commission (17–18% vs. 15%). Only enroll if the increased visibility generates meaningfully more bookings — test for 3 months and compare volume.
  - Response rate: respond to all guest inquiries within 1 hour. Booking.com measures response rate and penalizes properties below 90% in search ranking.
  - Review score management: see HM-009. A 0.1 increase in Booking.com review score (e.g., 8.7 to 8.8) typically increases conversion rate by 3–5%.
- **Airbnb** (strong for unique/boutique properties):
  - Listing style: storytelling over specification. "You'll wake up to the sound of birds and the smell of bread baking in our wood-fired oven" outperforms "Property features include Wi-Fi, parking, and breakfast."
  - Professional photos: Airbnb offers free professional photography in many markets. Use it.
  - Superhost status: maintain by meeting Airbnb thresholds (90%+ response rate, <1% cancellation rate, 4.8+ overall rating, 10+ stays per year). Superhost listings receive higher visibility and a trust badge.
  - Instant Book: enabling Instant Book improves search ranking but reduces your ability to vet guests. For a boutique property, consider enabling it to boost visibility while maintaining a clear house rules policy.
  - Pricing: Airbnb's Smart Pricing tool tends to underprice. Set your own prices per HM-005 and disable Smart Pricing.
- **Expedia** (strongest for international package travelers and corporate):
  - Useful if the corporate or international segment is a priority. Commission is higher (15–25%) but reaches a different audience than Booking.com.
  - For a small rural property, Expedia is lower priority than Booking.com and Airbnb unless international corporate demand is significant.
- **BookRetreats.com** (niche OTA for retreat/wellness travel):
  - Primary channel for the retreat segment. Listing should emphasize: natural setting, available practice/meeting spaces, dietary accommodation, group capacity, facilitator support.
  - Commission structure varies — negotiate based on volume commitment.
  - This is a high-value, low-volume channel. Each booking is typically multi-night and multi-room. Worth the management effort even if volume is small.
- **Metasearch** (Google Hotel Ads, TripAdvisor, Trivago):
  - Google Hotel Ads: connects your direct booking engine to Google search results. When a user searches "hotels in [your area]" and your property appears, the booking link goes directly to your site rather than to an OTA. This is the highest-intent, lowest-cost direct booking channel available. Set up through your booking engine provider (most integrate with Google Hotel Ads).
  - TripAdvisor Instant Booking: similar concept, though TripAdvisor's traffic has declined in recent years. Lower priority.
  - Trivago: useful in Europe for rate comparison visibility. Connect through your channel manager.

### Pas 4: Build and Protect the Direct Channel
- **Booking engine** (non-negotiable): your website must have a real-time booking engine — not a contact form, not an email inquiry button. A guest who clicks "Book Now" and is taken to a form instead of a checkout flow abandons 80% of the time. Booking engine providers: Cloudbeds (integrates PMS + booking engine + channel manager), SiteMinder (booking engine + channel manager), SimplerBooking, or Beds24.
- **Website quality**: the direct website is your storefront. Minimum requirements:
  - Mobile-optimized (60%+ of travel searches are mobile).
  - Load time under 3 seconds (test with Google PageSpeed Insights — every additional second of load time reduces conversion by 10%).
  - Professional photography prominently displayed.
  - Clear "Best Rate Guarantee" message: "Book directly for the best rate — guaranteed. Plus [specific benefit]."
  - Booking engine embedded on the homepage, not hidden behind navigation.
  - Room pages with photos, amenities list, and a "Book This Room" button.
  - Restaurant page with menu, photos, and reservation option.
  - Location page with map, directions, and travel tips.
  - Contact page with phone, email, and WhatsApp.
- **Direct booking incentives** (rate parity compliant — you cannot show a lower price than OTA, but you can add value):
  - Free breakfast (if not included in OTA rate).
  - Late checkout (14:00 instead of 11:00).
  - Room upgrade on availability.
  - Welcome drink or complimentary local product.
  - Free parking (if otherwise charged).
  - 10% discount on F&B during stay.
  - Display these incentives prominently on the website and at check-in for OTA guests (to convert them to direct for their next stay).
- **Email capture and re-marketing**: the most powerful direct booking tool.
  - Capture email at check-in (with GDPR consent): "May I have your email so we can send you exclusive offers and updates? We only send occasional emails — no spam."
  - Also capture from online inquiries, restaurant reservations (non-staying guests), newsletter signup on website, and social media contests.
  - Build the email list by segment (see HM-006).
  - Re-marketing sequence: (1) Post-stay thank-you email with direct booking offer within 24 hours of checkout. (2) Seasonal newsletter with new menu announcements, events, and exclusive packages (quarterly). (3) Anniversary email (12 months after their stay): "It's been a year since your visit — we'd love to welcome you back. Book directly for [benefit]."
  - Email conversion rate target: 3–5% of email recipients book a stay within 12 months. Even at 3%, a list of 1,000 email addresses generates 30 direct bookings per year at zero acquisition cost.
- **Social media as a booking channel** (Instagram, Facebook):
  - Instagram: with 12K followers, every post is a potential booking driver. Include "Link in bio" for booking. Use Instagram Stories for real-time content (fire cooking, sunset view, guest reactions). Use Reels for evergreen content (the full dining experience, morning at the property, behind-the-scenes kitchen prep). Tag the location consistently.
  - Facebook: create a booking button on the Facebook page linked to your direct booking engine. Run occasional retargeting ads to website visitors who did not book (Facebook Pixel required on website).
  - Track social media booking attribution: use UTM parameters on all social media booking links to measure exactly how many direct bookings originate from social channels.

### Pas 5: Corporate and B2B Channel Management
- **Corporate rate agreements**: identify companies within 100km that regularly need accommodation (tech companies, manufacturing plants, professional services firms). Approach them with a corporate rate proposal:
  - Offer: BAR-10 to BAR-15% (never more than BAR-20%).
  - Terms: valid for 12 months, minimum volume commitment (e.g., 30 room nights per year), invoicing with 30-day payment terms.
  - Process: email the proposal to the company's office manager, HR director, or travel coordinator. Follow up by phone within 1 week.
  - For the 107+ IT companies identified in the Albastru corporate outreach list: this is a direct sales opportunity. Even 10 companies booking an average of 20 room nights per year = 200 midweek room nights.
- **GDS (Amadeus/Sabre/Galileo)**: required if pursuing the managed corporate travel market (companies that book through a Travel Management Company/TMC). Connect through a GDS-enabled channel manager (SiteMinder, Pegasus). Commission: 10% to the TMC + GDS fees. This channel becomes relevant when corporate bookings exceed 15% of total revenue.
- **Tour operators and wholesalers**: bulk rates 20–30% below BAR. Guaranteed volume but lowest net ADR. Only pursue for specific low-demand periods. Typical terms: allotment of X rooms per week, release period (7–14 days before arrival), net rate invoiced monthly. Be cautious about allotments during high-demand periods — you are committing inventory at a discount that may sell at full price.
- **Local B2B partnerships**: partner with complementary businesses for mutual referral:
  - Event venues without accommodation (they refer overnight guests to you).
  - Wedding planners (wedding packages including accommodation block).
  - Local experience providers (wine tours, outdoor adventures — cross-promote).
  - Relocation agencies (long-stay temporary housing for relocating employees).
  - Referral commission: 10% of room revenue for referred bookings, tracked and paid monthly.

### Pas 6: Channel Manager and Technology Stack
- **Channel manager**: the software that distributes your rates and availability to all OTAs and your direct booking engine simultaneously. Essential when you are active on 3+ channels. Without a channel manager, you risk overbookings (selling the same room on two channels) and rate inconsistencies.
  - Recommended for small-mid properties: Cloudbeds (integrated PMS + booking engine + channel manager), SiteMinder (standalone channel manager), Beds24, eZee Centrix.
  - Cost: 50–200 EUR/month depending on property size and channels connected.
  - Key features required: real-time 2-way sync with Booking.com and Airbnb, automatic inventory adjustment when a booking is made on any channel, rate plan management with derived rates, reporting by channel.
- **Channel manager setup**: map each room type in your PMS to the corresponding room type on each OTA. Map each rate plan. Test by making a test booking and verifying it syncs correctly.
- **Inventory allocation strategy**: for properties with limited rooms (6–10), you generally list 100% of inventory on all channels simultaneously (the channel manager manages real-time availability). For larger properties, you may choose to hold back rooms from OTAs during peak demand (last-room availability control) to keep them available for direct or corporate.

### Pas 7: Channel Mix Review and Rebalancing (Monthly)
- Monthly KPIs to track:
  - **Direct Booking %**: target = grow by 5 percentage points year-over-year. Calculation: Direct Room Nights / Total Room Nights x 100%.
  - **Blended Commission Rate**: Total OTA Commission Paid / Total Room Revenue x 100%. This should decrease as direct share grows. Industry benchmark for independent hotels: 8–12% blended rate is healthy; above 15% means OTA dependency is too high.
  - **Channel Revenue Concentration**: if any single channel contributes >50% of bookings, you have a concentration risk. Diversify.
  - **Net ADR by channel**: compare monthly to ensure you are not systematically selling at lower net rates on specific channels.
- **Rate parity audit** (monthly): search for your property on Booking.com, Airbnb, and Expedia as a guest. Verify that displayed rates match across channels and that your direct channel offers the agreed value-add advantage. If a rate parity violation exists (guest can find a cheaper rate on one OTA than another), correct immediately — parity violations erode trust with both OTAs and direct guests.
- **Quarterly channel review**: re-rank channels by net revenue contribution. Consider deactivating underperforming channels that add management overhead without meaningful revenue. If a channel delivers fewer than 5 bookings per quarter and requires active management, the management cost likely exceeds the revenue benefit.

### Pas 8: Direct Booking Conversion Tactics at the Property
- Every OTA guest who checks in is a conversion opportunity for their next stay.
- **At check-in**: when you identify the booking source is OTA, note it. At the end of check-in, mention: "Next time you'd like to visit, if you book directly with us — by phone or our website — you'll get [free breakfast / late checkout / 10% F&B discount]. I'll put a card in your room with our direct booking details."
- **In-room**: place a "Book Direct" card on the desk or nightstand with the website URL, phone number, QR code linking directly to the booking engine, and the list of direct booking benefits.
- **At checkout**: repeat the direct booking invitation: "We hope to see you again. Remember, booking directly with us gets you [benefit]."
- **Post-stay email**: the most effective conversion touchpoint. Send within 24 hours of checkout with a direct booking link and an exclusive returning-guest benefit.
- **Track OTA-to-direct conversion rate**: of all OTA first-stay guests, how many return as direct bookers? Industry benchmark for well-executed conversion programs: 12–20% of OTA first-timers convert to direct on their second stay. This is the metric that justifies every direct channel investment.

## Note
- **Rate parity requirements**: Booking.com and Expedia contractually require that you do not display lower prices on other OTAs or your own website. However, you CAN offer better value (lower effective price through value-adds) on your direct channel. This is the legal and strategic mechanism for making direct booking more attractive. In the EU, some countries have legislated against rate parity clauses — check current Romanian legislation on OTA rate parity requirements.
- **Common mistake — opening too many channels**: more channels means more management, more rate parity risk, more cancellation processing, and more customer service spread across platforms. Focus on 2–3 OTAs plus your direct channel. Quality of presence on fewer channels outperforms mediocre presence on many.
- **Common mistake — ignoring OTA promotional tools**: Booking.com offers Visibility Boosters, Country Rate promotions, Mobile Rate promotions, and deal promotions. Each has a cost (higher commission or lower rate) but can significantly increase visibility during critical booking windows. Use them tactically for specific low-demand periods, not permanently.
- **Edge case — OTA account suspension**: if Booking.com or Airbnb suspends your listing (due to high cancellation rate, review score drop, or policy violation), you lose a major revenue channel instantly. Maintain compliance, respond to guest issues promptly, and always have a diversified channel mix so no single suspension is existential.
- **Albastru / Șapte Focuri context**: start with Booking.com + Airbnb + BookRetreats.com + direct (phone/email/Instagram DM). Add a website booking engine when direct volume justifies the monthly cost (typically when you have 3+ direct inquiries per week that could convert online). Prioritize Google Hotel Ads — it drives high-intent direct bookings at lower cost than OTA commission. For the Instagram audience (12K followers): every time you post food content, include a "Book your fire-kitchen dinner + stay" call to action. Instagram is the most natural direct booking driver for a visual, experiential property. With 17K Facebook followers, run occasional retargeting ads to people who visited your website but did not book — the retargeting audience is warm and converts at 5–10x the rate of cold audiences.

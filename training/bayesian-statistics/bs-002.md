# BS-002 — Compute Maximum Likelihood Estimates (MLE)

## Problema
Without MLE understanding, Bayesian practitioners cannot compare frequentist and Bayesian point estimates.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- R or Python
- calculus basics

### Steps
1. Write the likelihood function L(theta|data) for your model.
2. Take the log-likelihood: l(theta) = log L(theta|data).
3. Differentiate and set to zero to find the MLE analytically.
4. For complex models, use numerical optimization: `optim()` in R or `scipy.optimize`.
5. Compute standard errors from the Fisher information matrix.
6. Compare MLE with Bayesian posterior mean under flat prior.

### Verification
- MLE matches known analytical result for simple distributions.
- Numerical optimizer converges to same value.

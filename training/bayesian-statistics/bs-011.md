# BS-011 — Fit Bayesian Normal Model with Known/Unknown Variance

## Problema
The normal model is the workhorse of Bayesian statistics, appearing in regression, hierarchical models, and more.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- R or Python
- BS-004 completed

### Steps
1. Case 1 (known variance): specify Normal prior on mu, derive Normal posterior.
2. Compute posterior mean as weighted average of prior mean and sample mean.
3. Compute posterior precision as sum of prior and data precisions.
4. Case 2 (unknown variance): use Normal-Inverse-Gamma conjugate prior.
5. Derive joint posterior for (mu, sigma^2).
6. Compute marginal posterior for mu (Student-t distribution).

### Verification
- Known-variance posterior matches analytical formula.
- Unknown-variance marginal posterior follows t-distribution.

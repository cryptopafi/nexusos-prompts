# PB-001 — Conduct Market Segmentation Using the Iceberg Model

## Problema
Teams struggle to move beyond surface demographics and miss the deeper psychographic and behavioral layers that drive brand preference.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Access to customer data (surveys, CRM, analytics)
- Whiteboard or Miro board for mapping

### Steps
1. List all known demographic segments (age, income, location) as the visible tip of the iceberg.
2. Identify behavioral patterns beneath the surface: purchase frequency, channel preference, brand switching triggers.
3. Dig into psychographic drivers at the deepest layer: values, aspirations, fears, identity signals.
4. Map each segment on a 2x2 matrix of accessibility (easy to reach) vs. attractiveness (high LTV potential).
5. Prioritize 2-3 segments where your brand has permission to win.
6. Create a one-page segment profile for each priority segment including demographics, behaviors, psychographics, and media habits.
7. Validate segments with real customer interviews or survey data.

### Verification
- Segment profiles reviewed by at least one stakeholder
- Each profile contains all three iceberg layers
- Priority segments have clear sizing estimates

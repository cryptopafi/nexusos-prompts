# PB-002 — Build a Perceptual Map for Brand Positioning

## Problema
Brands often position themselves without understanding how consumers actually perceive them relative to competitors, leading to crowded or weak positioning.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Competitor list (5-8 brands)
- Consumer perception data or expert estimates
- Spreadsheet or mapping tool

### Steps
1. Select two key attributes that matter most to your target segment (e.g., price vs. quality, innovation vs. tradition).
2. List 5-8 competitor brands including your own.
3. Rate each brand on both attributes using consumer survey data or informed estimates (1-10 scale).
4. Plot all brands on a 2D map with each attribute as an axis.
5. Identify white space (unoccupied quadrants) and crowded zones.
6. Determine your ideal position based on segment priorities and white space opportunity.
7. Document the positioning gap between current perception and desired position.

### Verification
- Map includes all major competitors
- White space opportunities are labeled
- Current vs. desired position gap is documented

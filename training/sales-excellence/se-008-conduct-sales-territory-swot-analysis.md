# SE-008 — Conduct Sales Territory SWOT Analysis

## Problema
Reps work territories reactively, chasing whatever comes in rather than strategically targeting high-potential segments. Without a structured territory analysis, reps miss 40% of addressable market opportunities and over-invest in declining segments.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Territory assignment with defined boundaries (geographic, vertical, or named accounts)
- CRM with 12+ months of historical data (won/lost deals, revenue by account)
- Market data access (industry reports, competitive intelligence)
- Previous quarter's performance data

### Steps
1. **Define territory boundaries precisely** — Document: geographic scope (regions, cities, zip codes), industry verticals included, company size range (revenue/employee count), named/strategic accounts. If boundaries are ambiguous, clarify with your manager NOW — territory overlap causes internal competition and wasted effort.
2. **Conduct the Strengths analysis** — Pull CRM data and list: (a) Top 10 accounts by revenue and their retention rate, (b) Segments where your win rate exceeds 35%, (c) Product lines with highest margin in your territory, (d) Existing relationships with decision-makers (name them), (e) Unique advantages vs. competitors in this geography (local presence, language, industry expertise). Be specific — "good relationships" is not a strength; "direct access to VP Engineering at 8 of top 20 accounts" is.
3. **Conduct the Weaknesses analysis** — Identify: (a) Segments with <15% penetration rate, (b) Product lines you rarely sell (skill gap or fit gap?), (c) Accounts with declining revenue trend (>10% YoY decline), (d) Response time or service issues flagged by customers, (e) Competitive losses in last 6 months — to whom, why. Categorize each weakness as controllable (you can fix it) or structural (needs management/product intervention).
4. **Conduct the Opportunities analysis** — Research external factors: (a) Market segments growing >15% annually in your territory, (b) Competitor weaknesses (recent service failures, price increases, product gaps), (c) New product launches or features that open new segments, (d) Regulatory changes creating urgency (compliance deadlines), (e) Companies with recent funding, leadership changes, or M&A activity (trigger events). Use LinkedIn, Crunchbase, and industry publications. Target: 10+ specific opportunities.
5. **Conduct the Threats analysis** — Identify: (a) Competitors entering or expanding in your territory, (b) Market contraction in key segments, (c) Price pressure or commoditization trends, (d) Technology disruption affecting your buyers, (e) Customer consolidation reducing total addressable accounts. For each threat, estimate the revenue at risk if unaddressed over the next 12 months.
6. **Score and prioritize using the Impact/Actionability matrix** — For each SWOT item, rate: Impact on revenue (1-5) and Actionability (1-5: how much you can influence it). Plot on a 2x2 matrix. Focus on: High Impact + High Actionability quadrant first. Items that are high impact but low actionability need escalation to management.
7. **Convert SWOT to strategic priorities** — Select: 2 Strengths to double down on (defend and grow), 2 Weaknesses to address (fix or abandon), 3 Opportunities to pursue (with specific target accounts), 2 Threats to mitigate (defensive actions). Each priority gets an owner, target metric, and deadline.
8. **Build the 90-day Territory Action Plan** — For each priority, define: specific actions (not vague goals), target accounts or segments, required resources, milestones at 30/60/90 days, and success metric. Example: "Opportunity: RegTech segment growing 25%. Action: identify 15 RegTech companies in territory, research top 5, launch prospecting cadence by Day 15. Target: 3 qualified opportunities by Day 60."
9. **Present and align with management** — Schedule a 30-minute review with your manager. Present: territory overview, SWOT highlights, top 7 strategic priorities, 90-day plan with resource requests. Get alignment on priorities and commitment on resources (marketing support, SE time, pricing flexibility). Document agreed priorities.
10. **Review and refresh quarterly** — Every 90 days, score your plan execution: which priorities were achieved, which shifted, what new SWOT items emerged. Update the analysis, rotate in new priorities, retire completed ones. Track territory revenue growth vs. plan. Benchmark: territories with active SWOT plans grow 20-30% faster than reactive territories.

### Verification
- SWOT grid completed with 5+ specific items per quadrant
- Impact/Actionability matrix scored for all items
- 90-day action plan with 7+ priorities, specific accounts, and milestones
- Management alignment meeting completed
- Quarterly refresh scheduled

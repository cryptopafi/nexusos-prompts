# SE-004 — Use Open/Closed Questions to Uncover Client Needs

## Problema
Reps ask shallow questions and miss the real pain driving purchase decisions. Without structured questioning, discovery calls become product demos disguised as conversations, and reps solve problems the buyer never confirmed they had.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Discovery call or meeting scheduled with a prospect
- Basic understanding of SPIN Selling question framework
- CRM opportunity record created with pre-call research notes
- Prospect's website, LinkedIn, and recent news reviewed

### Steps
1. **Prepare a question map using SPIN categories** — Before the call, write 3 questions per SPIN category: Situation (facts about their current state), Problem (difficulties and dissatisfactions), Implication (consequences of the problem), Need-Payoff (value of solving it). Example: Situation: "How many leads does your team process monthly?" Problem: "Where do leads fall through the cracks?" Implication: "What does that cost you in lost revenue per quarter?" Need-Payoff: "If you could recover 30% of those leads, what would that mean for your pipeline?"
2. **Open with a broad situation question** — Start with: "Walk me through how [process] works at [Company] today." This lets the buyer talk, reveals structure, and shows you're interested in understanding before pitching. Listen for 2-3 minutes without interrupting. Take notes on specific terms they use — mirror their language later.
3. **Layer deep with the "3 Whys" technique** — After any answer, ask "Why is that?" or "Tell me more about that" at least 3 times. Surface-level: "We use spreadsheets for tracking." Layer 1: "Why spreadsheets vs. a CRM?" Layer 2: "What happens when the spreadsheet gets complex?" Layer 3: "How does that affect your team's forecasting accuracy?" The real pain is usually at layer 3.
4. **Use closed questions to confirm and quantify** — After uncovering a pain point, lock it down with closed questions: "So your team loses approximately 15 hours per week on manual data entry — is that right?" "Your current solution costs $X/year?" "The decision will be made by end of Q2?" Closed questions build a confirmed fact base for your proposal.
5. **Apply the "Impact/Priority" framework** — For each pain point uncovered, ask two questions: (a) "On a scale of 1-10, how much does this impact your business?" (b) "Where does solving this rank among your priorities this quarter?" If impact <7 and priority <top 3, the pain isn't strong enough to drive a purchase. Focus on pains scoring 8+ impact and top-3 priority.
6. **Listen for emotional vs. rational triggers** — Rational triggers: cost, efficiency, compliance. Emotional triggers: frustration, fear of failure, career risk, competitive anxiety. When you hear emotion ("I'm tired of..." or "My boss is pressuring me to..."), note it. Emotional triggers close deals; rational triggers justify them. Your proposal must address both.
7. **Map needs to your solution capabilities** — Create a 3-column need map during the call: Need (their words) | Priority (1-10) | Our Solution (specific feature/service). Only map needs you can actually solve. If you uncover a need you can't address, acknowledge it honestly — credibility matters more than coverage.
8. **Summarize and validate** — Before ending discovery, present your need map: "Let me make sure I captured this correctly. Your top three priorities are: [1], [2], [3]. You need a solution by [date], budget is approximately [X], and [Name] will make the final decision. Did I miss anything?" Get explicit confirmation or correction.
9. **Rate the discovery quality** — Self-score each discovery call: Did I speak less than 30% of the time? (benchmark). Did I uncover 3+ pain points? Did I reach layer-3 depth on at least one? Did I quantify the impact? Did I identify the decision process? Score 1 point each; target 4/5 minimum.
10. **Document and brief the team** — Within 30 minutes of the call, update CRM with: confirmed needs (buyer's exact words), priority ranking, decision process, timeline, budget range, and recommended next step. If account is strategic, brief your manager or SE with a 3-sentence summary.

### Verification
- SPIN question map prepared before each discovery call
- 3+ pain points uncovered and quantified per call
- Need map validated by prospect during call
- Discovery quality self-scored (target: 4/5)
- CRM updated within 30 minutes with buyer's own language

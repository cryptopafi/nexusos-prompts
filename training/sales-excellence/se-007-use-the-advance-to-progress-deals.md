# SE-007 — Use 'The Advance' to Progress Deals

## Problema
63% of deals stall because reps accept vague next steps ("I'll get back to you," "Send me some info") instead of securing concrete buyer commitments. Neil Rackham's research shows that the difference between top and average performers is the ability to gain "Advances" — specific buyer actions that move deals forward.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Active opportunity in pipeline with identified next milestone
- Understanding of your sales process stages and buyer journey (SE-001)
- CRM with activity tracking and next-step fields
- Authority to propose meetings, pilots, or trials

### Steps
1. **Distinguish Advances from Continuations** — An Advance is a concrete action by the BUYER that moves the deal forward: scheduling a demo with the CFO, providing access to their tech stack for integration review, introducing you to procurement. A Continuation keeps the conversation alive but doesn't progress: "Send me more info," "Let me think about it," "We'll circle back." Train yourself to recognize every continuation and upgrade it to an advance.
2. **Pre-plan your minimum acceptable advance** — Before every meeting, answer: "What is the smallest buyer action that would prove this deal is real?" Write it down. Examples by stage: Discovery → buyer shares internal requirements document. Demo → buyer brings technical evaluator to next call. Proposal → buyer confirms budget and decision timeline. Negotiation → buyer sends contract to legal. Never enter a meeting without knowing your minimum advance.
3. **Set the advance expectation at the meeting opening** — In the first 2 minutes: "By the end of today, I'd love for us to agree on [specific next step]. Does that seem reasonable?" This frames the meeting as outcome-oriented and gives the buyer a preview of the commitment you'll ask for. If they push back, you learn their resistance level early.
4. **Earn the advance during the meeting** — Advances are earned, not demanded. Deliver value proportional to what you're asking: if you want a meeting with the C-suite, you need to provide a compelling executive summary first. If you want access to their data for a pilot, you need to demonstrate the ROI potential. Match your ask to the value you've provided.
5. **Propose the advance with specificity** — Bad: "What should we do next?" (puts the burden on the buyer). Good: "Based on what we discussed, the logical next step would be a 30-minute call with your VP of Operations on Tuesday or Thursday. Which works better?" Always include: what the action is, who is involved, and a specific date/time. Vague advances are continuations in disguise.
6. **Handle the "continuation" response** — When the buyer says "Send me some info," respond: "Happy to. To make sure I send the right materials, could we schedule a 15-minute call for Thursday to walk through them together? That way I can tailor it to what matters most to you." You've accepted their request AND upgraded it to an advance.
7. **Use the "Commitment Ladder" for high-stakes advances** — If the advance you need feels too big (e.g., "Sign the contract"), break it into micro-advances: Step 1: "Can you share the procurement process?" Step 2: "Can we have a call with procurement to review terms?" Step 3: "Can procurement send their standard contract template?" Step 4: "Can you target signature by [date]?" Each micro-advance builds momentum and tests commitment.
8. **Track advance velocity in CRM** — Create a custom field: "Last Advance Date" and "Next Advance Due." Set a rule: if no advance is achieved within 14 days, the deal is flagged as stalled. Calculate your Advance Rate: (meetings resulting in an advance) / (total meetings). Benchmark: top performers achieve advances in 70%+ of meetings; average is 40%.
9. **Conduct weekly pipeline reviews focused on advances** — In every pipeline review, for each deal ask: "What was the last advance? What's the next advance? When is it due?" Deals without a clear next advance get a 48-hour window to secure one or get downgraded/removed from the pipeline. This discipline prevents pipeline bloat.
10. **Debrief every meeting on advance quality** — After each meeting, score: Did I get an advance? (Y/N). Was it the advance I planned? (Y/N). Is it a genuine buyer commitment or a polite continuation? (genuine/polite). What would I do differently? Track weekly advance rate trends. If your advance rate drops below 50%, revisit your value delivery and advance proposals.

### Verification
- Minimum advance planned before every meeting (written)
- Advance expectation set in opening of meetings
- Advance Rate tracked in CRM (benchmark: 70%+)
- Pipeline review uses advance-based criteria
- Stalled deals flagged at 14 days without advance

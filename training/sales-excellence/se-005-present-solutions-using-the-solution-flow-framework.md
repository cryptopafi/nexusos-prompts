# SE-005 — Present Solutions Using the Solution Flow Framework

## Problema
Sales presentations focus on features instead of connecting solutions to the buyer's specific problems. 74% of buyers choose the vendor who first adds value and insight (Forrester). Generic demos lose to competitors who map solutions to stated needs.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Completed discovery with documented needs (SE-004)
- Product/service knowledge sufficient to map features to outcomes
- Case studies or proof points available
- Presentation tool ready (PowerPoint, Google Slides, or live demo environment)

### Steps
1. **Review discovery notes and rank the top 3 needs** — Pull the need map from SE-004. Select the 3 highest-priority pain points (scored 8+ impact). These are the ONLY things your presentation should address. Resist the urge to show everything your product does — irrelevance kills attention.
2. **Build Solution Flows for each need** — For each of the 3 needs, construct: Need (restate in buyer's exact words) → Capability (your specific feature/service) → Benefit (quantified outcome) → Proof (case study, data, testimonial). Example: "You mentioned losing 15 hours/week on manual reporting [Need]. Our dashboard auto-generates reports from CRM data [Capability]. This typically saves teams 12+ hours/week [Benefit]. Here's how [Similar Company] reduced reporting from 20 hours to 3 [Proof]."
3. **Structure the presentation using the "Situation-Complication-Resolution" framework** — Open (2 min): restate their situation and goals to show you listened. Complicate (3 min): highlight what happens if they don't solve the problem (cost of inaction). Resolve (15 min): present 3 solution flows. Close (5 min): summarize and propose next step. Total: 25 minutes max, leaving time for questions.
4. **Personalize every slide** — Replace generic slides with their company name, their metrics, their use case. Use their industry terminology. Reference their competitors. Show mockups with their data if possible. A presentation that could be given to any prospect is a presentation that will convince no prospect.
5. **Use the "Pause and Confirm" technique** — After each Solution Flow, pause and ask: "Does this address what you described about [specific pain]?" or "How does this compare to your current approach?" This turns a monologue into a dialogue, keeps the buyer engaged, and surfaces objections early rather than at the end.
6. **Demonstrate, don't describe** — For software/tools: show a live demo of the exact workflow they'd use, ideally with their sample data. For services: walk through a timeline of what their first 90 days would look like. For products: use before/after comparisons. The buyer should be able to picture themselves using your solution.
7. **Handle the "What about..." questions** — When buyers ask about features you didn't cover, respond with: "Great question. That's something we do well, and I'd love to show you after we cover the three priorities you mentioned. Does that work?" This keeps the presentation focused on their stated needs rather than becoming a feature tour.
8. **Present pricing in context of value** — Never show pricing on a standalone slide. Always pair it with the ROI calculation: "The investment is $X/year. Based on the 15 hours/week savings we discussed, that represents $Y in recovered productivity — a [Z]x return in the first year." Frame it as investment vs. return, not cost.
9. **Create a leave-behind summary** — After the presentation, send a 1-page document: their 3 needs (their words), your 3 solution flows (short), the quantified ROI, and the proposed next step. This becomes the buyer's internal selling document when they present to their team. Make it easy for them to champion you internally.
10. **Collect feedback and iterate** — After each presentation, ask the buyer: "On a scale of 1-10, how well did this address your needs?" If <8, ask what was missing. Track presentation-to-proposal conversion rate (benchmark: 60-75%). Review every 10 presentations and update Solution Flows based on which proof points resonate most.

### Verification
- 3 Solution Flows built (Need → Capability → Benefit → Proof)
- Presentation personalized with buyer's company, data, and language
- "Pause and Confirm" used after each Solution Flow
- Leave-behind summary sent within 24 hours
- Presentation-to-proposal conversion tracked

# CS-002 — Eliminate Dominated Strategies in a Pricing Decision

## Version
2.0 — Opus v2 rewrite

## Problema
Firms waste resources evaluating pricing options that are objectively inferior regardless of competitor response. Without systematic elimination of dominated strategies, decision-makers suffer from choice overload and may select strategies that are never optimal. Iterative Elimination of Strictly Dominated Strategies (IESDS) narrows the strategic space to rational options, accelerating better pricing decisions.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Constructed game matrix with quantified payoffs (see CS-001)
- At least 3 strategy options per player (otherwise elimination is trivial)
- Payoff values validated with finance/revenue team using contribution margin data

### Steps
1. **Enumerate all feasible pricing strategies per player** — List every price point or pricing approach: premium, market-rate, discount, penetration, predatory, bundled, freemium. Include non-obvious options like dynamic pricing or auction-based mechanisms. Case: Airlines typically face {full fare, discount, deep discount, match competitor} strategy sets across route-level competition.
2. **Build the complete payoff matrix** — Populate payoffs for all strategy combinations using (price - unit cost) * expected volume. Factor in cross-price elasticity: your volume depends on BOTH your price and competitor's price. Use Porter's Value Chain to identify true cost basis. BCG Matrix logic applies: high-share players have experience-curve cost advantages that shift payoffs in their favor.
3. **Test for strict dominance (Round 1)** — For Player 1, compare each strategy row against every other row. Strategy A strictly dominates Strategy B if A's payoff > B's payoff in EVERY column (every possible competitor response). Eliminate all strictly dominated strategies. Repeat for Player 2 (columns). Case: In commodity markets, pricing above the competitive price is strictly dominated — you lose all volume with no offsetting margin gain.
4. **Test for weak dominance** — Strategy A weakly dominates B if A >= B in all scenarios and A > B in at least one. Flag weakly dominated strategies but do NOT auto-eliminate — weak dominance elimination is order-dependent and can remove valid Nash Equilibria. Document the judgment call with rationale.
5. **Iterate elimination (IESDS rounds)** — After removing dominated strategies, the reduced matrix may reveal NEW dominated strategies previously hidden. Repeat steps 3-4 on the smaller matrix. Continue until no further elimination is possible. Track each round in a log:
   ```
   | Round | Eliminated | Dominated By | Rationale |
   |-------|-----------|--------------|-----------|
   | R1    | P1: Deep discount | P1: Moderate | Lower margin, no share gain |
   | R2    | P2: Premium | P2: Market-rate | After R1 reduction, premium never wins |
   ```
6. **Analyze the reduced strategy space** — Count surviving strategies. If only one per player survives: dominant strategy equilibrium (strongest recommendation). If 2-3 survive: proceed to Nash Equilibrium analysis (CS-003). If none eliminated: payoffs may be too close — refine estimates or accept strategic complexity.
7. **Validate against real-world constraints** — Check if eliminated strategies serve purposes beyond the payoff matrix: brand signaling (premium pricing signals quality even if suboptimal on volume), regulatory compliance (minimum pricing laws), or relationship preservation. Flag as "dominated but strategically relevant" if applicable.
8. **Stress-test dominance under uncertainty** — Vary payoff estimates within confidence intervals (+/-20%). Use SWOT analysis to identify which external factors could flip dominance. If a strategy is dominated only under narrow assumptions, downgrade from "eliminate" to "deprioritize." PESTLE factors (e.g., new regulation, tech disruption) may invalidate current cost structures.
9. **Document and communicate the narrowed choice set** — Present the reduced matrix to decision-makers showing: original strategy count, elimination rationale per round, surviving options, and recommended next analysis (NE identification). This transforms a complex multi-option decision into a focused 2-3 option choice.

### Verification
- Every eliminated strategy has documented proof of dominance (which strategy dominates it, across which scenarios)
- Iteration log shows progressive reduction with no logical errors
- Weak dominance cases flagged separately from strict dominance
- Reduced matrix preserves all Nash Equilibria from original
- Stress test confirms dominance holds under +/-20% payoff variation

## Metrics
- Completion: Strategy space reduced by at least 30% through elimination
- Quality: Zero incorrectly eliminated strategies (verified by counter-example check)
- Speed: Decision time reduced by narrowing options from N to N-k

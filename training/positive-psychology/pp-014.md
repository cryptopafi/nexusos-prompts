# PP-014 — Create a Negative Visualization Practice

## Problema
Stoic negative visualization (premeditatio malorum) counteracts hedonic adaptation and builds resilience, complementing positive psychology practices.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Basic understanding of Stoic philosophy (optional)

### Steps
1. Choose a regular time for negative visualization practice (morning or evening, 5-10 minutes)
2. Visualize losing something you currently take for granted (health, relationship, job, home)
3. Sit with the feeling of loss for 1-2 minutes without rushing to reassurance
4. Return attention to the present and notice renewed appreciation for what you have
5. Practice for different life domains across the week
6. Combine with gratitude journaling to reinforce the appreciation effect
7. Limit practice to prevent excessive worry: structured exercise only, not rumination

### Verification
- Practice completed at least 3 times per week for 3 weeks
- Increased appreciation reported for at least one previously taken-for-granted element

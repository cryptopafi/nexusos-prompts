# DPM-025 — Draft Jobs-To-Be-Done Statements

## Problema
Product requirements are written as features instead of customer outcomes, leading to solutions that miss the mark.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Customer research data
- Understanding of JTBD framework

### Steps
1. Identify the main job your customer hires your product to do
2. Write the core JTBD statement: 'When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome]'
3. Break down the main job into 5-8 sub-jobs (steps in the process)
4. For each sub-job, identify: current solution, satisfaction level, importance to customer
5. Find underserved jobs: high importance + low satisfaction = biggest opportunity
6. Prioritize product features based on which underserved jobs they address
7. Validate JTBD statements with 5 customer interviews

### Verification
- Core JTBD statement written
- Sub-jobs mapped with satisfaction scores
- Underserved jobs prioritized

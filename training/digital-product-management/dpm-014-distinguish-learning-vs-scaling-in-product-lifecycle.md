# DPM-014 — Distinguish Learning vs Scaling in Product Lifecycle

## Problema
Teams try to scale products that haven't been validated, or keep testing products that should be scaling.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Product metrics
- Product-market fit data
- Team capacity information

### Steps
1. Define the Learning phase: searching for product-market fit, running experiments, iterating fast
2. Define the Scaling phase: PMF confirmed, optimizing unit economics, growing user base
3. Create clear transition criteria: 'Move to Scaling when [retention rate > X%, NPS > Y, unit economics positive]'
4. Assess your current product against the transition criteria
5. If in Learning: focus on speed of experiments, small team, low burn rate
6. If in Scaling: focus on reliability, growth channels, team expansion, process maturity
7. Communicate the current phase to all stakeholders so expectations are set correctly

### Verification
- Learning and Scaling phases defined
- Transition criteria documented
- Current phase assessed and communicated

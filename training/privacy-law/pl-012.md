# PL-012 — Conduct GDPR Compliance Gap Analysis

## Problema
GDPR non-compliance carries fines up to 4% of global annual revenue, making gap analysis a critical business risk exercise.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- PL-001 completed
- PL-002 completed

### Steps
1. Confirm GDPR applicability (EU establishment, or offering goods/services to, or monitoring behavior of, EU data subjects)
2. Map all processing activities and identify lawful bases (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public interest, legitimate interests)
3. Assess data subject rights implementation: access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, objection, automated decision-making
4. Evaluate consent mechanisms for GDPR validity (freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous, withdrawable)
5. Review data protection impact assessments (DPIA) for high-risk processing
6. Assess international data transfer mechanisms (SCCs, adequacy decisions, BCRs)
7. Evaluate Data Protection Officer (DPO) appointment requirement and compliance
8. Review data processing agreements with all processors

### Verification
- All Article 30 processing activities documented
- Lawful basis identified and documented for each processing activity

# PBR-001 — Choose a Personal Brand Niche

> **Version**: 2.0 | **Category**: Personal Branding — Positioning | **FORGE**: PASS

## Problema
Trying to brand yourself as a generalist dilutes recognition, slows audience growth, and makes monetization nearly impossible. A precisely chosen niche positions you as the go-to authority in a space people are actively searching for.

## Procedura

### Prerequisites
- Honest self-assessment of skills, passions, and market gaps
- Access to Google Trends, LinkedIn search, and audience research tools
- 2-3 hours of focused reflection time

### Steps

1. **Audit your expertise stack.** Open a Notion page titled "Niche Discovery." List your top 15 hard skills (technical abilities, certifications, professional experience) and 10 soft skills (communication style, teaching ability, unique perspectives). Rate each 1-5 for proficiency and 1-5 for enjoyment. Filter to items scoring 4+ on both.

2. **Map market demand signals.** For each surviving skill, search Google Trends (past 12 months), LinkedIn content search, and Reddit communities. Document monthly search volume, number of active communities, and whether the trend line is rising or flat. Discard anything with declining interest or zero community activity.

3. **Identify the intersection sweet spot.** Cross-reference your high-skill/high-passion items with high-demand topics. You need at least 3 items where all three dimensions overlap. If fewer than 3, broaden one skill category and repeat Step 2.

4. **Narrow to a micro-niche.** Take your top intersection topic and add two specificity layers: audience (who exactly?) and method (what unique approach?). Transform "marketing" into "AI-powered email marketing for B2B SaaS founders." The formula: [Method/Approach] + [Core Topic] + [Specific Audience].

5. **Run a competitive landscape scan.** Search LinkedIn, YouTube, and Twitter/X for creators in your proposed micro-niche. Find 5-15 active creators. If 0-4 exist, the niche may be too narrow (no proven demand). If 15+ exist, add another specificity layer. Document each creator's audience size, content format, and gaps they leave unfilled.

6. **Validate with real audience feedback.** Post a poll or question on LinkedIn and one relevant community (Slack group, Discord, subreddit). Ask: "If someone created [your niche content type], would you follow/subscribe?" Aim for 20+ positive responses. Use Calendly to book 3-5 short calls with interested respondents for deeper validation.

7. **Draft your niche positioning statement.** Write one sentence: "I help [specific audience] achieve [specific outcome] through [your unique method]." Test it by saying it aloud to 3 people outside your field. If they immediately understand what you do and who it is for, the statement works. If they ask clarifying questions, refine.

8. **Stress-test for longevity.** Ask yourself: Can I create 200+ pieces of content on this topic without repeating? Can this niche evolve with industry trends over 3-5 years? Does it allow for multiple monetization paths (consulting, courses, speaking, products)? If any answer is no, broaden slightly.

9. **Lock and document.** Finalize your niche in your Notion workspace. Create a "Brand Foundation" database entry with: niche statement, target audience persona, 3 competitive differentiators, and 5 content pillar topics. This becomes the anchor for every subsequent PBR procedure.

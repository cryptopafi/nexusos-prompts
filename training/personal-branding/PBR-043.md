# PBR-043 — Calculate and Optimize Viral Coefficient

## Problema
Growing only through direct content reach is linear. A viral coefficient above 1.0 means every user brings in more than one new user, creating exponential growth.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Active audience on at least one platform
- Analytics access

### Steps
1. Calculate your current viral coefficient: K = (invitations per user) x (conversion rate of invitations).
2. Measure invitations per user: on average, how many times does a follower share your content? (check shares/follower).
3. Measure conversion rate: of people who see shared content, what percentage follows you? (new followers from shares / total share views).
4. If K < 1.0 (sub-viral): focus on increasing shares per follower with more shareable content formats.
5. Test viral content mechanics: challenges, templates, fill-in-the-blank, tag-a-friend, save-and-share CTAs.
6. Track K weekly and A/B test: which content types produce the highest K value?
7. Goal: achieve K > 0.5 consistently (pure K > 1.0 is rare; even K = 0.3-0.7 dramatically accelerates growth).

### Verification
- Viral coefficient calculated with current data
- K value tracked weekly for 4 weeks
- Content types ranked by viral coefficient contribution

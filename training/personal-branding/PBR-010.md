# PBR-010 — Remove Negative Search Results

## Problema
Negative search results erode trust before you even get a chance to make an impression. Proactive suppression protects your brand equity.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- SEO baseline (PBR-009)
- Identification of negative results

### Steps
1. Document all negative results: URL, domain authority, content type, and whether removal is possible.
2. Attempt direct removal first: contact the site owner with a polite removal request citing specific reasons.
3. If the content violates platform policies, file a report with the platform (Google, social media, review site).
4. For defamatory content, consult legal options: DMCA takedown (for copyrighted content) or cease-and-desist.
5. If removal fails, suppress with positive content: create 10+ pieces of SEO-optimized content on high-DA platforms targeting the same keywords.
6. Accelerate suppression: build backlinks to your positive content, increase publishing frequency, and optimize for your name as a keyword.
7. Monitor monthly: negative results should drop from page 1 within 3-6 months of consistent effort.

### Verification
- All negative results documented with removal strategy
- Direct removal attempted for each result
- Suppression content plan active for any results that cannot be removed

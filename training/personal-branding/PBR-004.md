# PBR-004 — Select a Personal Brand Monetization Strategy

> **Version**: 2.0 | **Category**: Personal Branding — Monetization | **FORGE**: PASS

## Problema
Many personal brands build audiences but fail to monetize because they choose revenue models misaligned with their audience size, niche, and content format. Strategic monetization selection ensures revenue scales with brand growth.

## Procedura

### Prerequisites
- Completed niche selection (PBR-001) and brand ladder (PBR-002)
- Understanding of audience demographics and pain points
- Minimum 500 followers or email subscribers

### Steps

1. **Audit monetization readiness.** In Notion, create a "Monetization Assessment" page. Rate yourself 1-5 on: audience size, audience engagement (comments/replies per post), email list size, trust level (do people ask you for advice?), and content library depth. You need at least 3/5 on engagement and trust before monetizing — audience size alone is insufficient.

2. **Map the 7 personal brand revenue streams.** Document each in your Notion database: (1) Freelance/consulting services, (2) Digital products (courses, templates, ebooks), (3) Coaching/mentorship programs, (4) Speaking engagements, (5) Sponsorships and brand deals, (6) Affiliate marketing, (7) Community/membership subscriptions. For each, note: minimum audience needed, time investment, revenue potential, and scalability rating.

3. **Match revenue streams to your brand stage.** Foundation stage (0-1K): freelance services and affiliate links. Traction (1K-10K): add digital products and small-group coaching. Authority (10K-50K): add speaking, sponsorships, and premium courses. Influence (50K+): add memberships, book deals, and licensing. Select 1-2 streams appropriate for your current stage.

4. **Design your first paid offering.** Take your selected revenue stream and create a minimum viable offer. For consulting: define a specific deliverable, timeline, and price (use Calendly for discovery calls). For digital products: outline a course or template solving one specific problem. For coaching: design a 4-week program with weekly Loom video check-ins.

5. **Build a pricing framework.** Research 5 competitors in your niche and document their pricing. Position yourself using one of three strategies: (a) Premium — 20-50% above market; (b) Market rate — matching competitors; (c) Entry — 20-30% below market to build testimonials fast. Create a price increase roadmap: raise prices by 15-25% after every 10 clients or 50 product sales.

6. **Create a sales page or pitch deck.** Using Canva or your website builder, design a one-page sales page for your first offering. Include: headline with specific outcome, 3-5 bullet points of what is included, social proof, pricing, and a clear CTA (Calendly link for services, purchase button for products). Test with 5 people in your target audience.

7. **Set up payment and delivery infrastructure.** Choose a payment processor (Stripe, Gumroad, or Teachable depending on product type). Set up automated delivery: email sequences for digital products, Calendly + Loom workflows for services, community platform for memberships. Test the entire purchase-to-delivery flow yourself.

8. **Launch with a warm audience campaign.** Announce your offering to your existing audience. Use a 7-day launch sequence: Day 1 — problem awareness post; Day 3 — solution teaser; Day 5 — full announcement with early-bird pricing; Day 7 — deadline/scarcity. Send personalized DMs to your 20 most engaged followers.

9. **Track revenue metrics and optimize.** Create a Notion "Revenue Dashboard" tracking: monthly revenue per stream, conversion rate, average order value, customer acquisition cost, and lifetime value. Review weekly for the first month, then monthly. Kill revenue streams with less than 2% conversion after 90 days of optimization.

### Verification
- [ ] Monetization readiness assessment completed with honest ratings
- [ ] All 7 revenue streams documented with stage-appropriate selection made
- [ ] First paid offering designed with pricing, delivery, and target customer defined
- [ ] Pricing framework created with competitor research and increase roadmap
- [ ] Sales page or pitch deck created and tested with 5 target audience members
- [ ] Payment and delivery infrastructure tested end-to-end
- [ ] Revenue Dashboard set up in Notion with key metrics tracked

# PBR-002 — Create a Personal Branding Ladder

> **Version**: 2.0 | **Category**: Personal Branding — Strategy | **FORGE**: PASS

## Problema
Without a clear progression path, personal branding efforts become random acts of content. A branding ladder maps the journey from unknown to recognized authority, ensuring every action compounds toward the next level.

## Procedura

### Prerequisites
- Completed niche selection (PBR-001)
- Understanding of your current brand awareness level
- Notion workspace ready

### Steps

1. **Assess your current brand stage.** Rate yourself honestly across 5 dimensions: audience size (0-1K/1K-10K/10K-100K/100K+), content consistency (sporadic/weekly/daily), media mentions (zero/local/industry/national), monetization (none/side income/primary income), and peer recognition (unknown/emerging/established/authority). Plot these on a Notion table to identify your starting rung.

2. **Define the 5 ladder rungs with concrete milestones.** Create a Notion database "Brand Ladder" with stages: (1) Foundation — niche locked, profiles optimized, first 30 content pieces; (2) Traction — 1K followers, first collaboration, email list started; (3) Authority — 10K followers, guest appearances, first paid offering; (4) Influence — 50K+ followers, speaking invitations, media coverage; (5) Legacy — industry-defining content, book/course empire, mentorship program.

3. **Map specific actions to each rung.** For each stage, list 8-10 weekly activities. Foundation: 3 LinkedIn posts, 2 community comments, 1 blog article. Traction: add podcast guesting, newsletter launch, first Loom video series. Authority: add conference submissions, course creation, strategic partnerships. Document all in your Notion Brand Ladder.

4. **Set timeline gates.** Assign realistic timeframes: Foundation = months 1-3, Traction = months 4-9, Authority = months 10-18, Influence = months 19-36, Legacy = 36+. Add calendar milestones in your scheduling tool. Build in quarterly review checkpoints.

5. **Identify the leverage activities at each stage.** Not all actions are equal. At Foundation, the leverage activity is consistent publishing (volume builds skill). At Traction, it is collaborations (borrowed audiences). At Authority, it is premium content (courses/workshops). At Influence, it is media and speaking. Mark these as priority-1 in your Notion tracker.

6. **Build accountability systems.** Set up a weekly review ritual: every Sunday, log content published, engagement metrics, new connections, and revenue generated. Use a Notion template with rollups to track month-over-month growth. Share your ladder with one accountability partner who reviews your progress monthly.

7. **Create transition triggers.** Define the exact metrics that signal readiness for the next rung. Example: move from Foundation to Traction when you have 500+ followers, 30+ published pieces, and a consistent weekly publishing cadence for 8+ consecutive weeks. Do not advance prematurely — skipping rungs creates fragile brands.

8. **Design your content evolution plan.** Map how your content format and depth should change at each stage. Foundation: short-form text and simple graphics (Canva). Traction: add video (Loom) and newsletters. Authority: long-form guides, webinars, and case studies. Influence: keynotes, documentary-style content, and original research. Each stage demands new skills — schedule learning time.

9. **Document your unique brand story arc.** Write a 500-word narrative that connects your past experience, current niche expertise, and future vision. This story should explain WHY you are the right person for this niche. Use it across your LinkedIn About section, website bio, speaker applications, and podcast introductions. Refine it at each ladder transition.

### Verification
- [ ] Current brand stage accurately assessed across 5 dimensions
- [ ] 5-rung Brand Ladder created in Notion with concrete milestones
- [ ] Weekly activities mapped for current stage and next stage
- [ ] Timeline gates set with quarterly review checkpoints
- [ ] Leverage activities identified and marked priority-1
- [ ] Weekly review ritual scheduled and accountability partner confirmed
- [ ] Transition triggers defined with specific metrics

## Tools

- **LinkedIn** — Primary platform for professional brand building
- **Canva** — Visual content creation at each stage
- **Loom** — Video content starting at Traction stage
- **Calendly** — Scheduling accountability check-ins and collaborations

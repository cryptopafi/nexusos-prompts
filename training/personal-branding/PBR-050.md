# PBR-050 — Create a How-To Book Outline

## Problema
How-to books fail when they lack clear structure. A strong outline ensures the reader progresses logically from problem to solution.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Topic expertise
- Target reader defined

### Steps
1. Define the reader's starting point: what do they know and struggle with before reading your book?
2. Define the reader's end point: what will they be able to do after finishing your book?
3. Map the transformation journey: what are the 8-12 steps between starting point and end point? Each step becomes a chapter.
4. For each chapter: write the chapter title, a one-paragraph summary, and 3-5 key takeaways the reader will learn.
5. Add practical elements: exercises, templates, checklists, or worksheets at the end of each chapter.
6. Write the introduction last: it should promise the transformation and preview the journey.
7. Get feedback on the outline from 3 people in your target audience before writing the full book.

### Verification
- Reader starting and end points clearly defined
- 8-12 chapter outline with summaries and takeaways
- 3 target readers reviewed and approved the outline

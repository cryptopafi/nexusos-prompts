# PBR-044 — Create Viral Content Using Emotional Hooks

## Problema
Content goes viral when it triggers an emotional response strong enough to compel sharing. Understanding which emotions drive shares is a learnable skill.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Content creation capability
- Audience understanding (PBR-001)

### Steps
1. Study the 6 high-sharing emotions: awe, anxiety, anger, joy, lust, and surprise (Berger & Milkman research).
2. Audit your last 20 pieces of content: which emotion did each trigger? Correlate with share count.
3. Create an emotional hook library: 10 hook templates per emotion category.
4. For each new piece of content, choose one primary emotion and design the hook to trigger it.
5. Use the 'would I share this?' test before publishing: if you wouldn't share it yourself, the emotional hook is too weak.
6. Track share rates by emotion category over 4 weeks.
7. Double down on the emotion category that drives the highest share rate for your specific audience.

### Verification
- Emotional hook library created (60+ hooks across 6 emotions)
- Share rates tracked by emotion category
- Top-performing emotion category identified

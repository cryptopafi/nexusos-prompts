# PBR-041 — Set Up Home Recording Studio (Lighting + Audio)

## Problema
Poor lighting and bad audio make viewers click away regardless of content quality. A basic home studio dramatically improves production value.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Dedicated recording space (even a corner works)
- Budget for basic equipment

### Steps
1. Audio first (audio quality matters more than video): get a USB microphone (Blue Yeti, Rode NT-USB, or Fifine K669).
2. Reduce room echo: hang blankets on walls, add soft furnishings, or use a portable sound booth for the mic.
3. Key light: place your main light source in front of you, slightly above eye level and to one side (45 degrees).
4. Fill light: add a softer light on the opposite side to reduce shadows (a desk lamp with diffusion works).
5. Background: clean up or create a branded background. Bookshelves, plants, and subtle branding work well.
6. Camera position: eye level, centered. Use a webcam, smartphone on a tripod, or DSLR.
7. Test and record a 30-second clip: check audio levels, lighting balance, and background appearance.

### Verification
- USB microphone set up with echo reduction
- Two-light setup achieving even illumination
- Test recording reviewed and approved

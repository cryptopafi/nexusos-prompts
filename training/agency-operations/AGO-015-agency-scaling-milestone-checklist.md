# Agency Scaling Milestone Checklist

## Obiectiv
Navighează creșterea agenției prin 5 etape distincte — Solo ($0-$10K MRR) → Small Team ($10K-$50K) → Systemized ($50K-$100K) → Scaling ($100K-$200K) → Mature ($200K+) — cu checklist granular de condiții de trecere, team structure per etapă, financial benchmarks, și un map al death traps specifice fiecărui nivel — prevenind scalarea prematură (cauza #1 de regresie) și stagnarea din lipsa de viziune.

## Pași

### Pas 1: Identifică-te sincer în ce etapă ești — fără self-flattery

**ETAPA 1 — SOLO ($0-$10K MRR / $0-$120K ARR)**
```
Revenue: $0-$10,000 MRR
Clients: 0-5 active
Team: Founder + maybe 1 freelancer
Founder role: Does EVERYTHING (sales + delivery + admin + accounting)
Revenue per person: $5K-$10K/mo (mostly founder value)
Processes documented: Almost none
Time spent on delivery: 70-80%
Time spent on sales: 15-20%
Time spent on strategy: 5-10%

MAIN CONSTRAINT: Not enough clients OR not enough hours in the day
FEELS LIKE: "I'm drowning but also exhilarated"
```

**ETAPA 2 — SMALL TEAM ($10K-$50K MRR / $120K-$600K ARR)**
```
Revenue: $10,000-$50,000 MRR
Clients: 5-20 active
Team: 3-6 people (founder + 2-5 employees/contractors)
Founder role: Client management + sales + strategy + some delivery
Revenue per person: $7K-$12K/mo
Processes: Partially documented (onboarding, reporting exist)
Quality: Inconsistent (depends on who works on what)
Time spent on delivery: 40-50%
Time spent on client management: 30%
Time spent on sales: 15-20%

MAIN CONSTRAINT: Quality inconsistency + founder is bottleneck for everything
FEELS LIKE: "I'm managing people more than doing the work, but I still do the work"
```

**ETAPA 3 — SYSTEMIZED ($50K-$100K MRR / $600K-$1.2M ARR)**
```
Revenue: $50,000-$100,000 MRR
Clients: 15-35 active
Team: 7-15 people (delivery team + AM + sales + ops)
Founder role: Leadership + major sales + strategy
Revenue per person: $10K-$13K/mo
Processes: SOP for all major services, onboarding, reporting, billing
Roles: Clear — delivery, account management, sales are distinct functions
Quality: Consistent due to SOPs and QA processes
Time spent on delivery: 10-15% (only strategic oversight)
Time spent on leadership: 40%
Time spent on sales: 25%
Time spent on strategy: 20%

MAIN CONSTRAINT: Founder still key decision-maker for everything
FEELS LIKE: "The machine works but it needs me to keep it running"
```

**ETAPA 4 — SCALING ($100K-$200K MRR / $1.2M-$2.4M ARR)**
```
Revenue: $100,000-$200,000 MRR
Clients: 25-60 active
Team: 15-30 people with department structure
Founder role: CEO — vision, partnerships, major accounts, culture
Revenue per person: $12K-$15K/mo
Departments: Sales, Delivery, Operations/Finance, People
Leadership team: Ops Director/COO, Head of Delivery, Head of Sales
Process maturity: Formalized, audited, continuously improved
Quality: High and consistent, not dependent on any single person
Time spent on operations: 10-15% (via leadership team)
Time spent on strategy + partnerships: 50%
Time spent on major accounts: 20%
Time spent on culture + team: 15%

MAIN CONSTRAINT: Culture at scale + maintaining quality through growth
FEELS LIKE: "I'm working ON the business, and it works without me daily"
```

**ETAPA 5 — MATURE ($200K+ MRR / $2.4M+ ARR)**
```
Revenue: $200,000+ MRR
Clients: 50+ active (or fewer enterprise at $15K-$50K+/mo each)
Team: 25-50+ people, multi-level management
Founder role: Visionary CEO or has stepped back
Revenue per person: $13K-$18K/mo
Organization: P&L per department or service line
Leadership: C-suite (COO, CMO/CRO, VP Delivery)
Board/advisors: Optional but common
Exit potential: 3-6x annual revenue (for pure agency), 6-10x (for SaaS-hybrid)

MAIN CONSTRAINT: Innovation, market positioning, retention of A-players
FEELS LIKE: "It's a real company now"
```

### Pas 2: Checklist Etapa 1 → 2 (Solo → Small Team)

**Prerequisites (ALL must be met before hiring)**:
- [ ] ≥3 retainer clients paying consistently for ≥2 months each
- [ ] MRR ≥$10,000 for 2 consecutive months (not sporadic revenue)
- [ ] SOW template functional, all clients on signed contracts
- [ ] Onboarding process documented (even if basic) — AGO-003
- [ ] Cash reserve: 3 months of personal expenses + overhead
- [ ] At least 1 productized service with SOP — AGO-010
- [ ] First hire identified: role, JD, scorecard written — AGO-011
- [ ] Cost floor calculated for all services — AGO-006
- [ ] Time audit completed (where do YOUR hours go?)
- [ ] Referral from at least 1 existing client received

**Actions at transition**:
- Hire first execution specialist (contractor preferred — lower risk): $1,500-$3,000/mo
- Setup weekly team sync (30 min) — even with 1 hire, start the habit
- Activate project management tool (ClickUp or Asana — not email for task management)
- Start time tracking (Harvest/Toggl) — you need data for pricing decisions
- Setup financial tracker: P&L monthly + cash flow weekly — AGO-012

**Death trap at this stage**: hiring before revenue stability. Rule: don't hire until MRR covers hire cost × 3 months in cash reserve. If you hire at $10K MRR and hire costs $3K/mo, you need $9K in reserve beyond your personal needs.

### Pas 3: Checklist Etapa 2 → 3 (Small Team → Systemized)

**Prerequisites**:
- [ ] MRR ≥$50,000 for 3 consecutive months
- [ ] Gross Margin consistently ≥55%
- [ ] Revenue per employee ≥$8,000/mo
- [ ] SOP-uri for top 5 services (delivery + QA documented) — AGO-010
- [ ] Account Manager handles client communication without founder daily
- [ ] Reporting semi-automated: max 45 min per report — AGO-007
- [ ] Health Score per client implemented and reviewed monthly — AGO-014
- [ ] Client acquisition funnel generating ≥10 leads/month without founder as sole source — AGO-002
- [ ] Monthly churn rate <5% for 3 consecutive months
- [ ] No single client >25% of revenue
- [ ] Change Order process active and used — AGO-008
- [ ] Founder not involved in daily delivery execution

**Actions at transition**:
- Hire or promote Delivery/Operations Manager ($5K-$8K/mo) — most critical hire
- Document ALL remaining processes (if team member can't do it with SOP alone, SOP is incomplete)
- Activate P&L tracker with client profitability — AGO-012
- Launch QBR program for all retainer clients — AGO-007
- Implement formal hiring process (4-stage) — AGO-011
- Start building employer brand (LinkedIn, "life at agency" content)

**Death trap at this stage**: scaling clients without scaling processes. For every 3 new clients, add or improve 1 SOP. Quality degradation at this stage = churn explosion 3-6 months later.

### Pas 4: Checklist Etapa 3 → 4 (Systemized → Scaling)

**Prerequisites**:
- [ ] MRR ≥$100,000 for 3 consecutive months
- [ ] EBITDA Margin ≥20% sustained
- [ ] Revenue per employee ≥$10,000/mo
- [ ] No single client >20% of revenue
- [ ] Sales function operates without founder in discovery calls (founder closes only)
- [ ] Delivery Manager supervises team without founder in daily loop
- [ ] 90-day cash flow projection updated monthly — AGO-012
- [ ] Client NPS average ≥8
- [ ] Annual retention rate ≥80%
- [ ] Net Revenue Retention ≥105%
- [ ] Culture and values documented and practiced — AGO-011
- [ ] Multiple service lines with positive unit economics each
- [ ] Financial reporting: monthly P&L, weekly cash flow, client profitability

**Actions at transition**:
- Structure departments formally: Sales, Delivery, Operations/Finance
- Hire or promote: Head of Sales ($6K-$10K/mo + commission), Head of Delivery ($6K-$9K/mo)
- Create leadership meeting cadence: weekly leadership sync + monthly strategy
- Evaluate whether current niche can support $200K+ MRR or if diversification is needed
- Build public brand: case studies, speaking engagements, content marketing, Clutch/G2 presence
- Consider: SaaS revenue stream via GHL — AGO-009 (accelerates to $200K+ MRR)
- Formalize compensation structure: salary bands, performance bonuses, promotion criteria

**Death trap at this stage**: founder can't let go of control. If you're still reviewing every deliverable, attending every client call, and making every hiring decision, you ARE the bottleneck. Hire the leadership team BEFORE you feel ready — by the time you feel ready, you're already 6 months late.

### Pas 5: Checklist Etapa 4 → 5 (Scaling → Mature)

**Prerequisites**:
- [ ] MRR ≥$200,000 for 3 consecutive months
- [ ] EBITDA Margin ≥25%
- [ ] Revenue per employee ≥$12,000/mo
- [ ] Leadership team in place (COO/Ops Director + Sales Lead + Delivery Lead)
- [ ] Founder can be absent for 2+ weeks with no operational impact
- [ ] Formal hiring pipeline: always be recruiting for key roles (never desperate-hiring)
- [ ] Client acquisition: multiple channels active (inbound + outbound + referral + partnerships)
- [ ] Revenue diversification: ≥3 service lines OR retainer + project + SaaS streams
- [ ] Annual retention rate ≥85%
- [ ] Brand recognition in niche: clients come to YOU, not just through outreach
- [ ] Financial sophistication: P&L by department, rolling forecasts, board-ready reports
- [ ] Mentor/advisor relationship established (someone who's built a bigger agency)

**Actions at transition**:
- Formalize C-suite: COO (operations), Head of Growth/CRO (revenue), VP Delivery
- Consider: advisory board (2-3 external advisors, quarterly meetings)
- Plan long-term: agency sale/exit? lifestyle business? growth to $5M+?
- Invest in innovation: new service lines, AI integration, proprietary tools
- Build management bench strength: every key role has a potential successor identified

**Death trap at this stage**: losing culture during rapid growth. Every new hire dilutes culture unless intentionally reinforced. Formal onboarding with values integration, monthly all-hands, and regular culture audits become essential.

### Pas 7: Greșelile de scaling specifice fiecărei etape — death trap map

**Stage 1 → 2: "Hire too early"**
- Symptom: hire at $5K MRR, burn through cash in 3 months, fire the hire, back to solo
- Prevention: MRR must cover hire cost × 3 months in cash. Use freelancers first. Hire only when you're turning away work.
- Recovery: if you've over-hired, cut fast and rebuild cash. Don't keep unprofitable staff hoping revenue will catch up.

**Stage 2 → 3: "Scale without systems"**
- Symptom: 15 clients with no SOPs. Quality varies wildly. Client complaints increase. Team confused about who does what.
- Prevention: for every 3 new clients, add 1 SOP. Don't grow clients without growing processes.
- Recovery: pause client acquisition for 30-60 days. Document everything. Fix quality. Then resume growth.

**Stage 3 → 4: "Founder bottleneck"**
- Symptom: founder reviews every deliverable, attends every call, makes every decision. Team waiting for approvals. Growth plateaus at $80-$100K MRR.
- Prevention: hire Ops Manager BEFORE you hit $80K MRR. Start delegating decisions, not just tasks.
- Recovery: radical delegation. Pick 5 things only you can do. Everything else: delegate TODAY. Accept 80% quality temporarily.

**Stage 4 → 5: "Culture dissolution"**
- Symptom: new hires don't understand the "way we do things." Quality drops. Star performers leave because culture changed. Client satisfaction declines.
- Prevention: formal values onboarding, mentorship program, cultural rituals (all-hands, team retreats, recognition)
- Recovery: pause hiring. Reassess values. Exit poor culture fits (even if competent). Rebuild from remaining A-players.

**Universal death trap: "Revenue vanity"**
- $100K MRR at 8% EBITDA = $8K profit/month (barely viable)
- $60K MRR at 28% EBITDA = $16.8K profit/month (2x more profitable)
- Revenue without margin is vanity. Margin is the business. Never sacrifice margin for growth.

### Pas 8: Implementează quarterly scaling review

- **Every 90 days**: evaluate against current stage checklist
- **Review framework** (60 min, founder + leadership team):
  1. (15 min) Financial review: MRR trend, margin, cash runway, key metrics
  2. (15 min) Checklist review: which prerequisites for next stage are met? Which are not?
  3. (15 min) Gap analysis: what's the #1 gap preventing transition to next stage?
  4. (15 min) Action plan: 1-2 quarterly objectives directly tied to transition prerequisites
- **Set specific, measurable quarterly goals** tied to stage progression:
  - "Increase MRR from $42K to $50K by EOQ" (stage 2→3 prerequisite)
  - "Document SOPs for 3 remaining services by [date]" (stage 2→3 prerequisite)
  - "Hire Account Manager by [date] and complete 30-day onboarding" (stage 2→3 action)
- **Do NOT rush to next stage** — check ALL prerequisites. Premature scaling amplifies every weakness. Better to dominate your current stage for an extra quarter than to advance with gaps.
- **Track transitions**: when did you enter current stage? How long have you been here? What caused delays?
- **The litmus test for readiness**: "Can I leave for 2 weeks and the business operates without decline?" If no, you're not ready for the next stage — regardless of revenue.

## Note
- **Scaling is not a linear race** — it's a staircase. You build the foundation at each level, then step up. Skipping stairs means falling.
- **Etapa 3 (Systemized) is the most critical and most underestimated** — many founders jump from Stage 2 energy to Stage 4 ambition without building Stage 3 systems. Result: expensive chaos.
- Revenue alone doesn't determine the stage — process maturity, team independence, and founder role are equally important indicators.
- **The best indicator of readiness**: "Can I disappear for 2 weeks without the business suffering materially?" If yes at your current stage, you're ready for the next.
- **Prefer $60K MRR at 28% EBITDA over $100K MRR at 8% EBITDA** — margin is the business, revenue is the vanity metric. Scaling unprofitable revenue just scales problems.
- **Agency valuation at exit** (if relevant): pure-play agency = 3-5x annual EBITDA. Agency with SaaS component (GHL) = 5-8x. Agency with proprietary technology = 8-12x. Building SaaS revenue isn't just about MRR — it's about exit multiple.
- **The founder's #1 job changes at each stage**: Stage 1 = sell and deliver. Stage 2 = hire and systematize. Stage 3 = lead and optimize. Stage 4 = strategize and delegate. Stage 5 = vision and culture. If you're doing Stage 1 work at Stage 3, that's why you're stuck.

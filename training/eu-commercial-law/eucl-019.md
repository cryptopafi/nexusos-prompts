# EUCL-019 — Identify Anti-Competitive Agreements (Art. 101 TFEU)

## Problema
Businesses enter into agreements (distribution, joint ventures, information sharing) without recognizing they may constitute prohibited anti-competitive agreements, risking fines of up to 10% global turnover.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-001 (legal system), basic understanding of market structure

### Steps
1. Apply **Art. 101(1) TFEU** elements: (a) agreement between undertakings, decision by association of undertakings, or concerted practice, (b) which may affect trade between Member States, (c) which has as its object OR effect the prevention, restriction, or distortion of competition
2. Classify the agreement type:
   - **Horizontal** (between competitors): price-fixing, market sharing, output limitation, bid-rigging — these are hardcore restrictions, presumed "by object" (no effects analysis needed)
   - **Vertical** (between different levels of supply chain): RPM (resale price maintenance), exclusive distribution, selective distribution, non-compete clauses
3. Apply the **"by object" test**: does the agreement reveal a sufficient degree of harm to competition by its very nature? (Expedia, C-226/11: object restrictions cannot be de minimis). Examples: price-fixing (ANIC, C-49/92P), market sharing (ICI v Commission, Dyestuffs, C-48/69)
4. If not "by object," apply the **"by effect" test**: analyse actual or potential effects on competition in the relevant market. Requires market definition (product + geographic)
5. Check the **Art. 101(3) TFEU exemption** (self-assessment since Regulation 1/2003): (a) efficiency gains, (b) fair share of benefit passed to consumers, (c) restriction indispensable, (d) no elimination of competition for substantial part of products
6. Check for applicable **Block Exemption Regulations** (see EUCL-020): VBER for vertical agreements, R&D BER, Specialization BER, Technology Transfer BER
7. Apply the **de minimis** doctrine (Commission Notice 2014/C 291/01): agreements between undertakings with combined market share below 10% (horizontal) or 15% (vertical) generally do not appreciably restrict competition — BUT never for hardcore restrictions
8. Document compliance: implement a **competition law compliance programme** covering training, agreement review, information exchange protocols, and whistleblower channels

### Verification
- Can identify the elements of Art. 101(1) in a given agreement
- Can distinguish "by object" from "by effect" restrictions
- Can apply the Art. 101(3) exemption criteria

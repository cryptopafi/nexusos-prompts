# EUCL-027 — EU Public Procurement Law

## Problema
Contracting authorities breach EU procurement rules by incorrect procedure selection or award criteria, while tenderers miss opportunities to challenge unlawful awards.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-001 (legal system), understanding of public sector purchasing

### Steps
1. Determine **applicable directive**: (a) Directive 2014/24/EU — classic public sector, (b) Directive 2014/25/EU — utilities (water, energy, transport, postal), (c) Directive 2014/23/EU — concessions. Defence/security: Directive 2009/81/EC
2. Check **thresholds** (revised biennially by Commission): as of 2024: works EUR 5,382,000; supplies/services EUR 140,000 (central government) or EUR 215,000 (sub-central); utilities EUR 431,000. Below thresholds: treaty principles of transparency, non-discrimination still apply (Telaustria, C-324/98)
3. Select the correct **procedure** (Art. 26-32 Directive 2014/24):
   - **Open** (Art. 27): any interested party may submit tender. Most transparent
   - **Restricted** (Art. 28): two-stage — selection then invitation to tender
   - **Competitive dialogue** (Art. 30): complex contracts, needs cannot be met by standard solutions
   - **Competitive procedure with negotiation** (Art. 29): specific conditions (e.g., bespoke/complex, prior open procedure failed)
   - **Innovation partnership** (Art. 31): R&D and subsequent purchase of resulting product
   - **Negotiated without publication** (Art. 32): extreme urgency, exclusive rights, no tenders received — strictly construed
4. Apply **award criteria** (Art. 67): most economically advantageous tender (MEAT) based on best price-quality ratio, cost (life-cycle costing), or price only. Sub-criteria must be linked to subject matter, weighted, published in advance
5. Apply **exclusion grounds** (Art. 57): mandatory (corruption, fraud, money laundering, terrorism, child labour, tax/social security evasion) and discretionary (bankruptcy, professional misconduct, conflict of interest, prior poor performance)
6. Implement **e-procurement** obligations: electronic communication mandatory (Art. 22). TED (Tenders Electronic Daily) for publication above thresholds. eCertis for evidence of exclusion/selection criteria
7. Apply **Remedies Directives** (89/665/EEC and 92/13/EEC, as amended by 2007/66/EC): standstill period (minimum 10 days from award decision notification), automatic suspension upon challenge, interim measures, damages. Ineffectiveness of contract for most serious violations
8. Check for **strategic procurement** requirements: environmental (green procurement criteria), social (Art. 18(2) — labour law compliance), reserved contracts for sheltered workshops (Art. 20)

### Verification
- Can determine the correct procedure type and threshold for a given contract
- Can structure compliant award criteria under MEAT
- Can advise a tenderer on remedies for unlawful procurement

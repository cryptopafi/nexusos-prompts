# EUCL-024 — EU Competition Law Enforcement

## Problema
Undertakings are unprepared for Commission investigations and do not understand enforcement powers, leniency programmes, or settlement procedures that can dramatically reduce penalties.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-019 (Art. 101), EUCL-021 (Art. 102)

### Steps
1. Understand **Commission powers under Regulation 1/2003**: sole power to apply Art. 101(3) exemption revoked — now decentralized. Commission and NCAs enforce Art. 101 and 102 in parallel via the European Competition Network (ECN)
2. **Investigative powers** (Art. 17-22 Regulation 1/2003): (a) sector inquiries, (b) requests for information (Art. 18 — binding decisions, fines for incorrect/misleading answers up to 1% turnover), (c) inspections (Art. 20 — "dawn raids": can seal premises, examine books, take copies, ask questions)
3. **Dawn raid protocol**: Commission officials arrive with inspection decision (Art. 20(4)). Company should: contact external counsel immediately, check scope of decision, assign team to shadow inspectors, do NOT obstruct (fines up to 1% turnover), maintain legal privilege for in-house counsel communications (AKZO Nobel, C-550/07P — EU law does NOT cover in-house LPP)
4. **Statement of objections** (Art. 27(1)): Commission sets out its case. Addressees have right to respond in writing and request oral hearing. Access to file (except business secrets and internal documents)
5. **Leniency programme** (2006 Leniency Notice, updated): first undertaking to reveal cartel to Commission receives **full immunity** from fines. Subsequent applicants receive **reductions**: first 30-50%, second 20-30%, others up to 20%. Must provide evidence of added value, cooperate fully, cease participation
6. **Settlement procedure** (Regulation 622/2008): parties acknowledge involvement and liability in exchange for **10% fine reduction**. Speeds up proceedings. Can be combined with leniency
7. **Fines** (Art. 23 Regulation 1/2003): up to **10% worldwide annual turnover** of the group. Calculated per 2006 Guidelines: percentage of affected sales (up to 30%) x duration + entry fee (15-25%). Aggravating/mitigating factors applied. Parental liability for subsidiaries (Akzo Nobel, C-97/08P: 100% ownership = presumption)
8. **Commitment decisions** (Art. 9 Regulation 1/2003): Commission accepts binding commitments from parties without finding an infringement — avoids fines but commitments are enforceable

### Verification
- Can advise on dawn raid response protocol
- Can explain the leniency programme's immunity and reduction tiers
- Can calculate the basic amount of a Commission fine

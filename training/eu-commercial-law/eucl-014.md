# EUCL-014 — Navigate the EU Services Directive 2006/123/EC

## Problema
Service providers do not leverage the Services Directive's simplification mechanisms and fail to challenge prohibited national requirements, leaving market access barriers in place.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-010 (free movement of services), EUCL-013 (establishment)

### Steps
1. Confirm the service falls **within scope**: any self-employed economic activity provided for remuneration (Art. 4). Check exclusions (Art. 2): financial services, transport, healthcare, audiovisual, gambling, social services, private security, notaries, bailiffs
2. For **establishment** (Chapter III): review national authorization schemes. Art. 9: authorization permitted only if non-discriminatory, justified by overriding reason of general interest, and proportionate (no less restrictive means). Art. 10: conditions must be clear, objective, transparent, accessible, published in advance
3. Identify **prohibited requirements** (Art. 14): (a) nationality/residency of provider, (b) prohibition on establishment in multiple Member States, (c) restrictions on choosing between branch/subsidiary/agency, (d) reciprocity conditions, (e) economic needs tests, (f) involvement of competitors in authorization decisions
4. Evaluate **requirements to be assessed** (Art. 15): quantitative/territorial limits, minimum tariffs, obligation to take specific legal form — Member States must notify Commission and justify
5. For **cross-border service provision** (Chapter IV, Art. 16): host state may only impose requirements that are non-discriminatory, necessary, proportionate. Provider subject to home state law in principle
6. Use **Points of Single Contact** (Art. 6): electronic one-stop-shop for completing all procedures and formalities. Check if Member State has functioning PSC (available at ec.europa.eu/growth)
7. Apply **administrative cooperation** provisions (Art. 28-36): Member States must assist each other via IMI (Internal Market Information System) for supervision of cross-border providers
8. Note **mutual evaluation** obligations (Art. 39): Commission reviews national requirements regularly for compatibility

### Verification
- Can identify whether a service falls within or outside the Directive's scope
- Can flag prohibited requirements under Art. 14
- Can guide a service provider through PSC procedures

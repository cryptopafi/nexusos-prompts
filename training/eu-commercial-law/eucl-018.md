# EUCL-018 — EU Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (2005/29/EC)

## Problema
Businesses unknowingly engage in commercial practices prohibited EU-wide (misleading claims, aggressive selling, black-listed practices), facing coordinated enforcement across all Member States.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-016 (consumer rights), understanding of B2C marketing

### Steps
1. Apply the **scope**: B2C commercial practices before, during, and after a transaction. Does NOT apply to B2B (separate national laws or Directive 2006/114/EC on misleading/comparative advertising between traders). Maximum harmonization — Member States cannot deviate
2. Apply the **general prohibition** (Art. 5): a practice is unfair if (a) contrary to professional diligence, AND (b) materially distorts the economic behaviour of the average consumer (or targeted group)
3. Assess **misleading actions** (Art. 6): false information or overall presentation deceiving the average consumer regarding: product existence/nature, main characteristics, price, need for service/repair, trader's nature/identity. Includes **confusion marketing** (Art. 6(2))
4. Assess **misleading omissions** (Art. 7): omitting, hiding, or providing material information in unclear/ambiguous/untimely manner. For invitations to purchase: must include main characteristics, trader address, price, delivery/payment, withdrawal right, complaint handling
5. Assess **aggressive practices** (Art. 8-9): harassment, coercion, undue influence impairing consumer's freedom of choice. Factors: timing, location, persistence, threatening language, exploiting misfortune, onerous barriers to exercising contractual rights
6. Apply the **black list** (Annex I) — 31 practices always considered unfair: (a) claiming to be signatory of code of conduct when not, (b) displaying trust mark without authorization, (c) bait advertising, (d) pyramid schemes, (e) claiming "limited time" falsely, (f) falsely claiming after-sales in another language, (g) creating false impression product can legally be sold, and others
7. Apply the **average consumer** benchmark (recital 18, CJEU in Gut Springenheide, C-210/96): reasonably well-informed, observant, circumspect. Adjust for targeted vulnerable groups (age, credulity, mental/physical infirmity)
8. **Enforcement**: national consumer protection authorities, coordinated via CPC Regulation (2017/2394). Penalties must be effective, proportionate, dissuasive. Omnibus Directive (2019/2161) added fines of at least 4% annual turnover for widespread infringements

### Verification
- Can classify a practice as misleading action, misleading omission, or aggressive practice
- Can identify black-listed practices from Annex I
- Can apply the average consumer test

# EUCL-030 — Digital Markets Act (Regulation 2022/1925)

## Problema
Large platforms designated as gatekeepers face new ex-ante obligations that prohibit established business practices, while business users and competitors fail to leverage DMA rights.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-021 (dominance concepts), EUCL-026 (digital competition)

### Steps
1. Identify **gatekeeper designation criteria** (Art. 3): (a) significant impact on internal market (turnover EUR 7.5B+ in EU or market cap EUR 75B+ and active in 3+ Member States), (b) operates core platform service (CPS) as important gateway (45M+ monthly active end users AND 10,000+ yearly active business users in EU), (c) entrenched and durable position (thresholds met in last 3 fiscal years). Presumption if quantitative thresholds met; can rebut
2. List **Core Platform Services** (Art. 2(2)): online intermediation services, search engines, social networking, video sharing, number-independent interpersonal communication, operating systems, web browsers, virtual assistants, cloud computing, online advertising
3. Apply **Art. 5 obligations** (self-executing, no further specification needed):
   - No combining personal data across CPSs without GDPR consent (Art. 5(2))
   - Allow business users to offer different terms on other channels (anti-parity, Art. 5(3))
   - Allow business users to promote offers outside platform (Art. 5(4))
   - No requiring use of gatekeeper's payment/identification service (Art. 5(7))
4. Apply **Art. 6 obligations** (susceptible of further specification):
   - No self-preferencing in ranking (Art. 6(5))
   - No preventing users from un-installing pre-installed apps (Art. 6(3))
   - Allow third-party app stores and sideloading (Art. 6(4))
   - Data portability for end users and business users (Art. 6(9)-(10))
   - FRAND access to search/social/advertising data (Art. 6(11))
   - Messaging interoperability (Art. 7) — phased: 1-to-1 text/images by Feb 2025, group messaging by Feb 2027
5. **Compliance**: gatekeepers must submit compliance report within 6 months of designation. Commission can open non-compliance proceedings, impose interim measures
6. **Enforcement**: Commission has exclusive enforcement. Fines: up to **10% global turnover** (20% for repeat infringements). Periodic penalties: up to 5% average daily turnover. Systematic non-compliance: structural remedies including divestiture (Art. 18)
7. **Market investigation** powers (Art. 16-18): Commission can investigate new CPSs, update obligations, address systematic non-compliance
8. Note **interaction with competition law**: DMA is without prejudice to Art. 101/102 TFEU. Commission and NCAs retain competition powers. DMA violations do not automatically constitute Art. 102 abuse, but may inform analysis

### Verification
- Can determine whether a platform qualifies as a gatekeeper
- Can list the specific obligations applicable to a designated gatekeeper's CPS
- Can distinguish DMA obligations from Art. 102 analysis

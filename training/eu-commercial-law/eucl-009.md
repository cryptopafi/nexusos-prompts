# EUCL-009 — Apply Free Movement of Goods (Art. 34-36 TFEU)

## Problema
Businesses face illegal trade barriers when Member States impose measures equivalent to quantitative restrictions, but fail to recognize which restrictions are prohibited and which are justifiable.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-001 (legal system), EUCL-006 (cross-border triggers)

### Steps
1. Identify a **measure having equivalent effect to a quantitative restriction (MEQR)** on imports using the **Dassonville formula** (C-8/74): any trading rule capable of hindering, directly or indirectly, actually or potentially, intra-EU trade
2. Apply the **Cassis de Dijon** principle (C-120/78): goods lawfully produced and marketed in one Member State must be admitted to other Member States (mutual recognition). Exceptions: mandatory requirements (consumer protection, fairness of commercial transactions, public health, environmental protection)
3. Apply the **Keck limitation** (C-267-268/91): selling arrangements (e.g., Sunday trading, advertising bans) are outside Art. 34 if they apply equally in law and fact to domestic and imported products
4. Check for **Art. 36 TFEU justifications**: public morality, public policy, public security, health/life protection, national treasures, IP protection. Must be proportionate and not arbitrary discrimination or disguised restriction
5. Assess **proportionality**: is the measure suitable to achieve the objective? Is it necessary (no less restrictive alternative)? Is it proportionate stricto sensu?
6. Check for **harmonized EU standards**: if sector is harmonized (e.g., CE marking, food safety regulations), mutual recognition gives way to compliance with EU harmonized rules
7. For **customs duties and charges having equivalent effect** (Art. 30 TFEU): absolutely prohibited, no justification possible (unless genuine service rendered — Bresciani, C-87/75)
8. For **discriminatory internal taxation** (Art. 110 TFEU): imported products must not bear higher taxes than similar domestic products

### Verification
- Can identify an MEQR using Dassonville/Cassis tests
- Can distinguish product requirements (caught by Art. 34) from selling arrangements (Keck)
- Can apply Art. 36 justifications and proportionality analysis

# EUCL-006 — Determine Whether EU Law Applies to a Cross-Border Situation

## Problema
Businesses and advisers fail to identify when a situation triggers EU internal market rules, missing both compliance obligations and available free movement rights.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-001 (legal system), familiarity with four freedoms (Art. 34-66 TFEU)

### Steps
1. Identify the **cross-border element**: EU free movement rules require a link between at least two Member States. Purely internal situations fall outside EU law (Morson and Jhanjan, C-35-36/82)
2. Classify the economic activity under the **correct freedom**: goods (Art. 34-36), workers (Art. 45-48), establishment (Art. 49-55), services (Art. 56-62), capital (Art. 63-66)
3. Confirm the person/entity is an **EU national or EU-established company** (Art. 54 TFEU: companies formed under Member State law with registered office, central administration, or principal place of business in EU)
4. Check for **reverse discrimination**: some Member States apply EU law even to purely internal situations; otherwise the national must rely on domestic law alone
5. Assess **third-country connections**: free movement of capital (Art. 63) uniquely covers third-country movements. For services/establishment with third countries, check bilateral agreements or EU FTAs
6. Identify potential **EU citizenship** triggers (Art. 20-21 TFEU): Zambrano (C-34/09) — even without cross-border movement, EU citizenship rights may apply if denial of genuine enjoyment of citizenship rights
7. Determine **temporal application**: EU law applies from accession date for newer Member States (check transitional arrangements for workers/capital from recent accessions)
8. Document the cross-border nexus for compliance records or litigation strategy

### Verification
- Can identify whether a given business situation has a sufficient cross-border element
- Can classify the applicable freedom correctly
- Can flag situations where EU law does not apply (purely internal)

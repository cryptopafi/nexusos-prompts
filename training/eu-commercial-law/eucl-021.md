# EUCL-021 — Identify Abuse of Dominant Position (Art. 102 TFEU)

## Problema
Dominant undertakings engage in conduct that exploits or excludes competitors without recognizing it as abusive, while smaller firms fail to identify actionable abuses against them.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-019 (competition basics), market definition methodology

### Steps
1. **Define the relevant market**: (a) product market — demand-side substitutability using SSNIP test (hypothetical monopolist 5-10% price increase), supply-side substitutability; (b) geographic market — area where conditions of competition are sufficiently homogeneous
2. **Assess dominance**: ability to behave independently of competitors, customers, and consumers (United Brands, C-27/76). Indicators: market share (above 50% = presumption per AKZO, C-62/86), barriers to entry, countervailing buyer power, access to resources/technology
3. Identify **exploitative abuses** (Art. 102(a)): unfair purchase/selling prices (United Brands — excessive pricing: price bears no reasonable relation to economic value), unfair trading conditions
4. Identify **exclusionary abuses**:
   - **Predatory pricing** (Art. 102(b)): prices below AVC presumed abusive (AKZO); below ATC abusive if part of plan to eliminate competitor
   - **Tying and bundling** (Art. 102(d)): making sale conditional on unrelated product (Microsoft, T-201/04)
   - **Refusal to deal/essential facilities**: refusal to supply where input is indispensable, refusal eliminates all competition, no objective justification (Bronner, C-7/97; IMS Health, C-418/01)
   - **Margin squeeze**: dominant vertically-integrated firm prices wholesale/retail such that efficient competitor cannot profitably compete (TeliaSonera, C-52/09)
   - **Exclusive dealing/rebates**: fidelity/loyalty rebates that lock in customers (Intel, C-413/14 P — now requires effects analysis)
5. Apply **objective justification** defence: dominant firm can show conduct is objectively necessary or produces efficiencies outweighing anti-competitive effects, with benefit passed to consumers
6. Note: **no exemption equivalent to Art. 101(3)** — but objective justification and efficiency defence serve similar function
7. Check for **collective dominance** (Airtours/Commission, T-342/99): two or more firms jointly dominant if tacit coordination sustainable
8. Consider **special responsibility**: dominant firms bear special responsibility not to impair undistorted competition (Michelin, C-322/81)

### Verification
- Can define the relevant product and geographic market
- Can assess dominance using market share and structural indicators
- Can classify conduct as exploitative or exclusionary abuse

# EUCL-008 — Navigate the EU Legislative Process

## Problema
Stakeholders miss lobbying windows and misunderstand the status of proposed legislation because they do not understand the EU's ordinary legislative procedure and its alternatives.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-002 (EU institutions)

### Steps
1. Identify the **legal basis** in the TFEU: this determines the legislative procedure (ordinary vs special) and voting rules in Council
2. **Ordinary Legislative Procedure (Art. 294 TFEU)** — most common, co-decision between Parliament and Council:
   - Commission proposal → Parliament first reading (amendments by simple majority)
   - Council first reading (accept Parliament text = adopted; amend = common position by QMV)
   - Parliament second reading (approve/amend/reject within 3 months)
   - Conciliation Committee (equal EP + Council) if disagreement → joint text within 6 weeks
   - Third reading: both approve joint text or act fails
3. **Special Legislative Procedures**: consultation (Parliament opinion, Council decides — e.g., Art. 113 TFEU taxation) or consent (Parliament veto power — e.g., Art. 218(6) TFEU international agreements)
4. Track proposals via **EUR-Lex procedure files** (inter-institutional procedure numbers: COD for ordinary, CNS for consultation, APP for consent)
5. Identify **lobbying windows**: most effective before Commission proposal (impact assessment stage) and during Parliament committee stage (rapporteur drafts report, shadow rapporteurs negotiate)
6. Monitor **trilogues**: informal negotiations between Parliament, Council, and Commission that produce most final texts — not formally in treaties but standard practice
7. Check for **delegated acts** (Art. 290 TFEU) post-adoption: Commission supplements non-essential elements, Parliament/Council can object within typically 2 months
8. Track implementation via **implementing acts** (Art. 291 TFEU) through comitology procedures (examination or advisory)

### Verification
- Can trace a legislative proposal from Commission initiative to final adoption
- Can identify the correct procedure type from the treaty legal basis
- Can pinpoint optimal lobbying intervention points

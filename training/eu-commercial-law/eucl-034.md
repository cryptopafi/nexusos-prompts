# EUCL-034 — EU Platform-to-Business Regulation (2019/1150)

## Problema
Business users of online platforms lack transparency about ranking, terms changes, and dispute resolution, putting them at a disadvantage in platform-dependent commercial relationships.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-001 (legal system), understanding of platform business models

### Steps
1. Identify **scope** (Art. 1): applies to online intermediation services (marketplaces, app stores, social media for business, booking/travel platforms, comparison tools) and online search engines. Provider must offer services to business users established in EU, offering goods/services to EU consumers. Regardless of provider's establishment
2. Implement **terms and conditions** requirements (Art. 3): plain and intelligible language, easily available at all stages. Must include: ranking parameters, grounds for suspension/termination, information on additional channels, IP policy, general data access description
3. Apply **restriction/suspension/termination** rules (Art. 4): provide statement of reasons on durable medium. For termination: 30 days advance notice (unless legal obligation, repeated infringement, or demonstrable risk). Business user must have opportunity to clarify facts before restriction
4. Ensure **ranking transparency** (Art. 5): set out main parameters determining ranking and reasons for their relative importance. For search engines: describe main ranking parameters and relative importance. Paid inclusion/ranking must be disclosed. No obligation to reveal algorithms but must describe factors
5. Disclose **differentiated treatment** (Art. 7): if platform provider also offers goods/services in competition with business users (vertical integration), disclose any differentiated treatment (ranking, data access, terms) given to provider's own offerings vs third-party offerings
6. Disclose **data access** practices (Art. 9): describe whether platform or business user has access to personal/non-personal data provided by or generated through the service. Describe conditions and scope of access
7. Implement **internal complaint handling** (Art. 11): platforms with >50 employees must establish internal system for business users. Process complaints expeditiously, communicate outcomes individually. Publish annual information on functioning of system
8. Facilitate **mediation** (Art. 12): identify mediators willing to resolve disputes. Engage in good faith during mediation. Organizations/associations representing business users have standing to bring actions for non-compliance (Art. 14)

### Verification
- Can identify services within scope of the P2B Regulation
- Can draft compliant terms including ranking transparency disclosure
- Can design an internal complaint handling system for a platform

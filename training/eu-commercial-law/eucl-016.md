# EUCL-016 — EU Consumer Rights Directive (2011/83/EU)

## Problema
Businesses selling to consumers cross-border fail to meet mandatory pre-contractual information and withdrawal rights, exposing themselves to enforcement actions and contract voidability.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-009 (free movement of goods), basic contract law understanding

### Steps
1. Determine **scope**: applies to contracts between traders and consumers (B2C). Covers distance contracts (online, phone), off-premises contracts, and on-premises contracts. Excludes: social services, healthcare, gambling, financial services, package travel, timeshare, real estate (separate directives)
2. For **distance/off-premises contracts**, provide mandatory **pre-contractual information** (Art. 6): main characteristics of product/service, trader identity and address, total price including taxes, delivery costs, payment/delivery/performance arrangements, withdrawal right details, complaint handling policy
3. Implement the **14-day withdrawal right** (Art. 9): starts from delivery of goods or conclusion of service contract. Consumer need not give reason. Trader must provide standard withdrawal form (Annex I(B))
4. Handle **withdrawal consequences** (Art. 13-15): refund all payments including standard delivery within 14 days of receiving withdrawal notice. May withhold refund until goods returned. Consumer bears return shipping costs only if informed in advance
5. Recognize **withdrawal exceptions** (Art. 16): sealed goods unsealed by consumer (hygiene), personalized goods, perishable goods, sealed audio/video/software unsealed, newspapers/periodicals, urgent repair services, digital content started with consent and acknowledgment of loss of withdrawal right
6. For **on-premises contracts** (Art. 5): reduced information obligations but still must provide main characteristics, identity, total price, complaint handling, and after-sales services
7. Apply **digital content** rules (Art. 16(m)): withdrawal right lost for digital content (non-tangible) once performance begun with consumer's express consent and acknowledgment. Must confirm consent on durable medium
8. Implement **button solution** for online orders (Art. 8(2)): order button must use words like "order with obligation to pay" or equivalent unambiguous formulation. Failure = consumer not bound

### Verification
- Can list all mandatory pre-contractual information items for distance contracts
- Can calculate the withdrawal period correctly in different scenarios
- Can identify applicable exceptions to the withdrawal right

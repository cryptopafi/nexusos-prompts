# EUCL-010 — Apply Free Movement of Services (Art. 56-62 TFEU)

## Problema
Service providers face unlawful barriers when expanding cross-border, and businesses fail to leverage the Services Directive to access other Member State markets efficiently.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-006 (cross-border triggers), distinction between services and establishment

### Steps
1. Define **services** (Art. 57 TFEU): provided for remuneration, temporary in nature, not covered by goods/capital/workers provisions. Includes professional, financial, construction, tourism services
2. Identify the **cross-border scenario**: (a) provider goes to recipient's state, (b) recipient goes to provider's state (Luisi and Carbone, C-286/82), (c) service crosses border (digital/remote), (d) both move to third state
3. Detect **restrictions**: any national measure making cross-border service provision less attractive is caught (Sager, C-76/90). Includes authorization requirements, residency conditions, professional qualifications mandates
4. Apply **justifications**: overriding reasons of general interest (public policy, consumer protection, professional rules, environmental protection). Must pass proportionality test
5. Apply the **Services Directive 2006/123/EC**: (a) prohibited requirements Art. 14 (nationality, residency, economic needs tests), (b) requirements to be evaluated Art. 15 (quantitative limits, tariff obligations), (c) freedom to provide services Art. 16
6. Use **Points of Single Contact** (Art. 6 Services Directive): online portals where service providers complete all administrative procedures for cross-border provision
7. For **posted workers**: apply Directive 96/71/EC (as amended by 2018/957) — host state minimum terms (wages, working time, health/safety) apply. Posting must be notified via host state system
8. Check sector **exclusions** from the Services Directive: financial services, transport, healthcare, gambling, social services, private security, temporary work agencies

### Verification
- Can distinguish temporary service provision from establishment
- Can identify prohibited requirements under the Services Directive
- Can advise on posted workers obligations

# EUCL-011 — Apply Free Movement of Capital (Art. 63-66 TFEU)

## Problema
Cross-border investments and capital transfers face unlawful restrictions that businesses fail to challenge, and the unique third-country dimension of capital movement is often overlooked.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-006 (cross-border triggers)

### Steps
1. Identify **capital movements** covered by Art. 63 TFEU (using Directive 88/361/EEC Annex I as interpretive guide): direct investments, real estate, securities, loans, guarantees, personal capital movements
2. Recognize the **unique scope**: Art. 63 TFEU prohibits restrictions on capital movements between Member States AND between Member States and third countries — the only freedom with this external dimension
3. Detect **restrictions**: any national measure liable to deter, impede, or render less attractive cross-border capital movements. Includes: golden share arrangements (Commission v Portugal, C-367/98), prior authorization for investments, discriminatory tax treatment
4. Apply **justifications** (Art. 65 TFEU): (a) distinction between resident/non-resident taxpayers if not arbitrary discrimination (Art. 65(1)(a)), (b) prudential supervision of financial institutions, (c) public policy and public security (narrowly construed)
5. Note the **third-country standstill clause** (Art. 64(1) TFEU): existing restrictions on capital to/from third countries involving direct investment, establishment, financial services, or securities may be maintained if they existed on 31 Dec 1993 (or 31 Dec 1999 for Bulgaria, Estonia, Hungary)
6. Assess **real estate acquisitions**: restrictions on agricultural land purchases by non-residents tested frequently (Ospelt, C-452/01 — proportionality required)
7. For **tax matters**: distinguish between justified anti-avoidance measures and unjustified discrimination. CJEU consistently strikes down exit taxes without deferral options (National Grid Indus, C-371/10)
8. Consider interaction with **EU anti-money laundering** framework and **FDI Screening Regulation** (2019/452) for third-country investments in strategic sectors

### Verification
- Can identify capital movement restrictions caught by Art. 63 TFEU
- Can apply the third-country dimension unique to capital movement
- Can assess golden share and real estate restriction cases

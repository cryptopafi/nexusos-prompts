# EUCL-017 — EU Product Liability (Directive 85/374/EEC, updated 2024)

## Problema
Manufacturers, importers, and distributors underestimate strict liability exposure for defective products, leading to inadequate risk management and insurance coverage across EU markets.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-015 (CE marking), understanding of tort/delict law basics

### Steps
1. Apply **Directive 85/374/EEC** (as revised by Directive 2024/2853 — new Product Liability Directive): **strict liability** — no need to prove fault. Producer liable for damage caused by defective product
2. Define **defective product** (Art. 6 original / updated criteria): product does not provide the safety a person is entitled to expect, considering presentation, reasonably foreseeable use, time of circulation. New directive adds: product safety requirements, effect on vulnerable groups, product's ability to learn/evolve (AI/software)
3. Identify the **liable person**: (a) manufacturer of finished product, (b) manufacturer of component part, (c) any person presenting themselves as producer (own-label), (d) **importer** into the EU. New directive adds: **online marketplace** operators and **fulfilment service providers** where manufacturer not identifiable/established in EU
4. Under the **2024 update**: software and AI systems are explicitly covered as "products"; digital manufacturing files included; continuous updates to products can trigger liability
5. Claimant must prove: (a) **defect**, (b) **damage**, (c) **causal link**. New directive introduces **disclosure of evidence** mechanism and **presumption of defectiveness** if claimant faces excessive difficulty proving defect (e.g., technical complexity, trade secrets)
6. Apply **defences** (Art. 7): product not placed on market by defendant, defect did not exist when product put into circulation, **development risk defence** (state of scientific/technical knowledge at time — optional for Member States), component manufacturer defence (defect attributable to design of finished product)
7. **Limitation periods**: action within 3 years from awareness of damage/defect/identity of producer. Absolute cut-off: 10 years from circulation (extendable to 25 years for latent personal injury under new directive)
8. **Damages covered**: death, personal injury, damage to property for private use above threshold (EUR 500 under old directive; threshold removed in 2024 update). New directive adds: data loss/corruption, psychological harm (medically recognized)

### Verification
- Can identify all potentially liable parties in a product liability claim
- Can apply the defectiveness test under both old and new directive
- Can advise on available defences and limitation periods

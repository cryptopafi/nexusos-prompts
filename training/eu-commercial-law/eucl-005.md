# EUCL-005 — Bring an Action Before the CJEU

## Problema
Practitioners miss enforcement opportunities by not knowing which CJEU procedure to use for challenging EU acts, enforcing EU law against Member States, or obtaining authoritative interpretations.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-001 (legal system), EUCL-002 (institutions)
- Familiarity with CJEU Rules of Procedure

### Steps
1. **Preliminary Reference (Art. 267 TFEU)**: national court refers question of EU law interpretation/validity to CJEU. Courts of last instance MUST refer (CILFIT exceptions: acte clair, acte eclaire). Draft the reference with precise questions on interpretation
2. **Action for Annulment (Art. 263 TFEU)**: challenge legality of EU acts. Check standing: (a) privileged applicants (Member States, institutions) — automatic; (b) non-privileged (natural/legal persons) — must show direct and individual concern (Plaumann test, C-25/62) or challenge regulatory act of direct concern not entailing implementing measures
3. **Action for Failure to Act (Art. 265 TFEU)**: if EU institution fails to act despite obligation. Must first call on institution to act; if no response within 2 months, bring action within further 2 months
4. **Infringement Proceedings (Art. 258-260 TFEU)**: Commission or Member State (Art. 259) against a Member State breaching EU law. Phase 1: letter of formal notice → Phase 2: reasoned opinion → Phase 3: CJEU referral. Art. 260: financial penalties for non-compliance with judgment
5. **Plea of Illegality (Art. 277 TFEU)**: incidental challenge to a regulation's legality in proceedings before CJEU, even after time limit for annulment has passed
6. **Damages Action (Art. 340 TFEU)**: non-contractual liability of EU institutions. Must show (a) unlawful conduct, (b) actual damage, (c) causal link (Bergaderm, C-352/98 P)
7. Observe **time limits**: annulment 2 months from publication/notification; use General Court for direct actions by natural/legal persons, CJEU for appeals on points of law

### Verification
- Can select the correct CJEU procedure for a given factual scenario
- Can assess standing for annulment actions under Plaumann and post-Lisbon rules
- Can draft a preliminary reference question

# EUCL-032 — EU Data Act (Regulation 2023/2854)

## Problema
Manufacturers and service providers fail to implement data access and portability requirements for IoT and connected products, losing compliance and missing B2B data-sharing opportunities.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-037 (GDPR fundamentals), understanding of IoT/connected products

### Steps
1. Understand **scope** (Art. 1): applies to data generated by connected products (IoT devices, machines, vehicles) and related services. Supplements GDPR — personal data subject to both regimes. Applies from 12 September 2025
2. Implement **user access rights** (Chapter II, Art. 3-7): manufacturers must design products so data is accessible to users by default, easily, securely, free of charge. Users can access and use their data, and share it with third parties
3. Handle **B2B data sharing** (Chapter III, Art. 8-12): data holders must make data available to third parties at user's request. Terms must be fair, reasonable, non-discriminatory (FRAND). Compensation: reasonable, transparent, related to costs of making data available. Cannot use data to compete with user's product/service
4. Protect against **unfair contractual terms** (Chapter IV, Art. 13): terms unilaterally imposed on micro/SMEs regarding data access are unfair if they grossly deviate from good commercial practice. Non-exhaustive list includes: excluding liability for intentional acts, limiting remedies, giving data holder sole right to interpret terms
5. Apply **B2G data sharing** (Chapter V, Art. 14-22): public sector bodies can request data from private holders in cases of exceptional need (public emergencies, official statistics). Data used only for stated purpose. Compensation limited to costs (free for public emergencies)
6. Implement **cloud switching** rights (Chapter VI, Art. 23-31): cloud providers must eliminate switching barriers. Customers entitled to: port data/digital assets/applications to other provider, minimum 30-day functional equivalence period, switching charges reduced to zero (by 12 Jan 2027). Contractual minimum standards for interoperability
7. Apply **international data access safeguards** (Chapter VII, Art. 32): providers must take reasonable technical, legal, organizational measures to prevent unlawful non-EU government access to non-personal data held in EU. Transfers only if international agreement or enforceable decision from court with mutual legal assistance treaty
8. Note **interaction with other instruments**: GDPR prevails for personal data; trade secrets protected (data holder can agree on technical protective measures); database rights (sui generis right does not apply to data generated by connected products, Art. 43)

### Verification
- Can identify data access obligations for a connected product manufacturer
- Can structure FRAND terms for B2B data sharing
- Can advise on cloud switching rights and timelines

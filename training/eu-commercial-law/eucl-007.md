# EUCL-007 — Transpose an EU Directive into National Law

## Problema
Member States face infringement proceedings when directives are not correctly or timely transposed, and businesses face legal uncertainty during the transposition gap period.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-001 (legal system), EUCL-002 (institutions), EUCL-003 (direct effect for untransposed directives)

### Steps
1. Identify the **transposition deadline** in the directive's final articles (typically 18-24 months from entry into force). Note that regulations require NO transposition — they apply directly
2. Conduct an **implementation gap analysis**: compare existing national law with the directive's requirements article by article. Identify provisions that need new legislation, amendments, or administrative measures
3. Choose the correct **national legal instrument**: primary legislation (act of parliament), secondary legislation (ministerial decree/order), or administrative guidance — depending on national constitutional requirements
4. Ensure **faithful transposition**: Member States must achieve the directive's result but may choose form and methods (Art. 288 TFEU). However, must not use transposition to reduce existing higher standards unless directive is a maximum harmonization measure
5. Distinguish **minimum harmonization** (Member States can go beyond, e.g., Consumer Rights Directive allows stricter national rules in some areas) from **maximum harmonization** (no divergence permitted, e.g., UCPD 2005/29/EC)
6. Notify the Commission via the **TRIS database** (Technical Regulations Information System) for technical standards, or through the **national transposition measures (MNE)** notification system
7. Monitor for **Commission infringement proceedings** (Art. 258 TFEU): failure to notify = automatic letter of formal notice. Incorrect transposition triggers substantive review
8. During the **transposition gap** (directive adopted but not yet transposed): advise clients that (a) vertical direct effect may apply after deadline, (b) national courts must interpret existing law in conformity with directive (Marleasing), (c) Francovich damages available for non-transposition

### Verification
- Can identify transposition deadline and applicable national instrument
- Can distinguish minimum vs maximum harmonization
- Can advise on legal position during transposition gap

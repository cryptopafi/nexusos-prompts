# EUCL-025 — Private Enforcement of Competition Law

## Problema
Victims of anti-competitive conduct fail to claim damages before national courts, missing compensation opportunities created by the EU Damages Directive framework.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-019 (Art. 101), EUCL-024 (public enforcement)

### Steps
1. Apply **Directive 2014/104/EU** (Damages Directive): right of any natural or legal person who has suffered harm caused by competition law infringement to claim full compensation (actual loss + loss of profit + interest)
2. Use **follow-on claims**: Commission or NCA final infringement decision constitutes **irrebuttable proof** of infringement before national courts of the same Member State (Art. 9(1)). Decision of another Member State's NCA: at least prima facie evidence (Art. 9(2))
3. Apply the **presumption of harm for cartels** (Art. 17(2)): cartels are presumed to cause harm (overcharge). Infringer may rebut this presumption. Courts can estimate quantum of harm (Art. 17(1))
4. Access **evidence**: national courts can order disclosure from defendant or third parties (Art. 5-8). Proportionality test applies. Leniency statements and settlement submissions protected from disclosure (Art. 6(6)) but other documents in Commission file available after proceedings closed
5. Apply **limitation periods** (Art. 10): at least 5 years from when claimant knew or should have known of infringement, harm, and identity of infringer. Suspended during Commission/NCA investigation. Fresh period of at least 1 year after infringement decision becomes final
6. Apply **passing-on defence and indirect purchaser claims** (Art. 12-16): defendant can argue overcharge was passed downstream. Indirect purchasers can claim if they prove passing-on reached them. Presumption of passing-on to indirect purchasers (Art. 14). Courts must avoid overcompensation across the chain
7. **Joint and several liability** (Art. 11): each infringer liable for full harm. Internal contribution rights between co-infringers. SME exception: liable only to own direct/indirect purchasers if market share below 5% and full liability would jeopardize viability. Immunity recipient: liable only to own purchasers unless others cannot obtain full compensation elsewhere
8. **Quantification**: Commission published Practical Guide on Quantifying Harm (2013). Methods include: before-and-after, yardstick/benchmark, cost-based, regression analysis. Expert economists typically required

### Verification
- Can structure a follow-on damages claim using a Commission/NCA decision
- Can advise on disclosure rules including leniency document protection
- Can explain the passing-on defence and indirect purchaser standing

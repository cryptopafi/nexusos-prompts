# EUCL-013 — Apply Freedom of Establishment (Art. 49-55 TFEU)

## Problema
Companies and self-employed persons face barriers to setting up in other Member States and struggle with corporate mobility rules, losing competitive advantages from EU establishment rights.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-006 (cross-border triggers), distinction from services (temporary vs permanent)

### Steps
1. Define **establishment** (Art. 49 TFEU): stable and continuous participation in economic life of a Member State — includes setting up agencies, branches, subsidiaries, or primary establishment
2. Confirm **personal scope** (Art. 54 TFEU): companies formed under any Member State's law with registered office, central administration, or principal place of business in the EU are treated as natural persons who are nationals of Member States
3. Identify **restrictions**: any national measure hindering or making less attractive the exercise of establishment. Includes: authorization requirements, qualification conditions, legal form requirements, shareholding restrictions
4. Apply **Gebhard test** (C-55/94): non-discriminatory restrictions justified only if (a) applied in non-discriminatory manner, (b) justified by imperative requirements of general interest, (c) suitable, (d) proportionate
5. Apply **company law mobility** case law: Centros (C-212/97) — cannot refuse registration of branch of company validly formed in another MS. Inspire Art (C-167/01) — cannot impose domestic law requirements on pseudo-foreign companies. Polbud (C-106/16) — cross-border conversion of registered office is exercise of establishment right
6. Apply the **Cross-Border Mergers Directive (2017/1132, codified)**: procedure for merging companies across Member States. Extended by **Directive 2019/2121** (cross-border conversions, mergers, divisions)
7. Navigate the **public authority exception** (Art. 51 TFEU): activities connected with exercise of official authority — narrowly interpreted, must be direct and specific participation in public authority
8. Check for **harmonized professional qualifications**: Directive 2005/36/EC (as amended) — mutual recognition, automatic recognition for 7 professions (doctors, nurses, dentists, pharmacists, architects, vets, midwives)

### Verification
- Can distinguish establishment from service provision
- Can apply the Gebhard four-part test
- Can advise on cross-border company mobility using Centros/Inspire Art/Polbud

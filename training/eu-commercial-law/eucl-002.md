# EUCL-002 — Identify EU Institutions and Their Legislative Roles

## Problema
Misunderstanding which EU institution holds which legislative, executive, or judicial power leads to incorrect lobbying targets, procedural errors, and flawed compliance strategies.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Familiarity with EUCL-001 (EU Legal System fundamentals)
- Access to TEU Articles 13-19

### Steps
1. Map the **European Commission** (Art. 17 TEU): sole right of legislative initiative, guardian of treaties, enforces EU law, negotiates international agreements
2. Map the **European Parliament** (Art. 14 TEU): directly elected, co-legislator under ordinary legislative procedure (Art. 294 TFEU), budgetary authority, democratic oversight
3. Map the **Council of the EU** (Art. 16 TEU): represents Member State governments, co-legislator, adopts by QMV (55% states + 65% population) or unanimity depending on legal basis
4. Map the **European Council** (Art. 15 TEU): heads of state/government, sets political direction, no legislative function
5. Map the **Court of Justice of the EU (CJEU)** (Art. 19 TEU): Court of Justice + General Court, ensures uniform interpretation, preliminary references (Art. 267 TFEU)
6. Identify key **regulatory agencies**: ECB (monetary policy, banking supervision via SSM), ESMA (securities markets), EBA (banking), EIOPA (insurance/pensions)
7. Understand the **European Court of Auditors** (Art. 285-287 TFEU): audits EU finances
8. For any legislative proposal, trace the institutional pathway: Commission proposal → Parliament first reading → Council position → conciliation if needed

### Verification
- Can identify which institution initiates, amends, and adopts EU legislation
- Can determine the correct voting procedure (QMV vs unanimity) for a given treaty article
- Can name the relevant regulatory agency for a specific sector

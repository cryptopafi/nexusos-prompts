# EUCL-023 — State Aid Rules (Art. 107-109 TFEU)

## Problema
Public authorities grant aid without Commission approval and companies receive subsidies that may later be clawed back, creating massive financial exposure and legal uncertainty.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-001 (legal system), understanding of public finance

### Steps
1. Apply the **four cumulative criteria** of Art. 107(1) TFEU — measure constitutes State aid if: (a) granted by State or through State resources (including tax exemptions, guarantees, loans below market rate), (b) confers a selective advantage (favours certain undertakings/production), (c) distorts or threatens to distort competition, (d) affects trade between Member States
2. Apply the **Market Economy Operator Principle (MEOP)**: if the State acts as a rational private investor would, the measure is not aid (Commission v EDF, C-124/10P)
3. Check for **automatic compatibility** (Art. 107(2) TFEU): aid of social character to individual consumers, natural disaster aid, aid to areas affected by German division
4. Check for **discretionary compatibility** (Art. 107(3) TFEU): (a) regional development aid, (b) common European interest/serious economic disturbance, (c) certain economic activities/areas (most commonly used)
5. Apply **de minimis Regulation** (Regulation 2023/2831): aid below EUR 300,000 per undertaking over 3 fiscal years not considered State aid. Separate de minimis for SGEI (EUR 750,000), agriculture (EUR 25,000), fisheries (EUR 40,000)
6. Apply the **General Block Exemption Regulation (GBER — Regulation 651/2014, as amended 2023)**: pre-approved categories of aid not requiring notification — includes regional aid, SME aid, R&D&I, training, environmental/energy, broadband, culture, sport. Must meet specific conditions and notification thresholds
7. **Notification obligation** (Art. 108(3) TFEU): all aid not covered by GBER or de minimis must be notified to Commission BEFORE implementation. Standstill obligation until Commission decision
8. **Recovery of unlawful aid** (Art. 16 Regulation 2015/1589): Commission orders recovery of incompatible aid with interest. National courts must enforce. No legitimate expectations defence for unnotified aid (Deggendorf, C-355/95P)

### Verification
- Can apply the four-criteria test to identify State aid
- Can determine whether GBER or de minimis applies
- Can advise on notification obligations and recovery risk

# EUCL-020 — Apply Block Exemption Regulations

## Problema
Businesses miss the safe harbour provided by EU block exemption regulations, either restructuring agreements unnecessarily or failing to notice when their arrangements fall outside exemption.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-019 (Art. 101 fundamentals)

### Steps
1. Understand the **mechanism**: Block Exemption Regulations (BERs) declare that categories of agreements satisfying specified conditions are automatically exempt from Art. 101(1) under Art. 101(3). No notification required since Regulation 1/2003
2. Apply the **Vertical Block Exemption Regulation (VBER) — Regulation 2022/720** (replaced 2010/330):
   - Applies to vertical agreements (different levels of supply chain) where supplier market share AND buyer market share each below **30%**
   - **Hardcore restrictions** (Art. 4) — never exempt: RPM (minimum/fixed resale prices), absolute territorial protection, restriction of active/passive online sales (with nuanced rules for selective distribution)
   - **Excluded restrictions** (Art. 5): non-compete obligations exceeding 5 years, post-term non-compete
   - New: **dual distribution** rules (Art. 2(4)-(6)) — horizontal information exchange limits; **online sales parity clauses** (wide MFN banned, narrow MFN exempt)
3. Apply **R&D BER (Regulation 2023/2485)**: agreements between competitors for joint R&D where combined market share below 25%. Must grant access to results for independent exploitation
4. Apply **Specialization BER (Regulation 2023/2486)**: unilateral/reciprocal specialization and joint production where combined market share below 20%
5. Apply **Technology Transfer BER (Regulation 316/2014)**: patent/know-how licensing. Competing undertakings: 20% combined share. Non-competing: 30% individual share. Hardcore restrictions include output restrictions, territorial/customer allocation (with exceptions)
6. Check for **Motor Vehicle BER (Regulation 461/2010)**: specific rules for aftermarket parts and repair services
7. If agreement falls **outside BER**: not automatically prohibited — must individually assess under Art. 101(1) and potentially self-assess under Art. 101(3)
8. Monitor **withdrawal of benefit**: Commission (Art. 29(1) Regulation 1/2003) or national competition authorities (Art. 29(2)) can withdraw BER benefit in individual cases

### Verification
- Can determine which BER applies to a given agreement type
- Can identify market share thresholds for each BER
- Can spot hardcore restrictions that prevent BER application

# EUCL-001 — Navigate the EU Legal System

## Problema
Practitioners struggle to distinguish between EU legal instruments (treaties, regulations, directives, decisions) and their binding force, leading to misapplication of EU law in cross-border commercial matters.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Basic understanding of international law concepts
- Access to EUR-Lex (eur-lex.europa.eu) for primary and secondary legislation

### Steps
1. Identify the **primary law** hierarchy: TEU (institutional framework, Art. 1-55) and TFEU (substantive policies, Art. 1-358) form the constitutional basis of EU law
2. Distinguish secondary legislation instruments:
   - **Regulations** (Art. 288 TFEU): directly applicable in all Member States, no transposition needed (e.g., GDPR Regulation 2016/679)
   - **Directives** (Art. 288 TFEU): bind as to result, require national transposition within deadline (e.g., Services Directive 2006/123/EC)
   - **Decisions** (Art. 288 TFEU): binding on addressees only (e.g., Commission merger decisions)
3. Apply the **direct effect** doctrine (Van Gend en Loos, C-26/62): EU provisions create rights enforceable by individuals if clear, precise, unconditional
4. Apply the **supremacy** principle (Costa v ENEL, C-6/64): EU law prevails over conflicting national law, including constitutional provisions (Internationale Handelsgesellschaft)
5. Check for **delegated acts** (Art. 290 TFEU) and **implementing acts** (Art. 291 TFEU) that supplement or implement the base legislation
6. Use EUR-Lex to trace the full legislative chain: treaty basis → regulation/directive → delegated/implementing acts → CJEU interpretation

### Verification
- Can correctly classify any EU legal instrument by type and binding force
- Can identify the treaty basis (TFEU article) for a given piece of secondary legislation
- Can explain when direct effect applies to regulations vs directives

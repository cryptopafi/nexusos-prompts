# EUCL-012 — Apply Free Movement of Workers (Art. 45-48 TFEU)

## Problema
Employers and workers fail to assert or comply with EU rights on non-discrimination, equal treatment, and social advantages in cross-border employment situations.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-006 (cross-border triggers)

### Steps
1. Define **worker** under EU law (Lawrie-Blum, C-66/85): a person performing services for and under the direction of another in return for remuneration. No minimum hours/salary threshold (Levin, C-53/81: part-time work counts if genuine and effective)
2. Apply **Art. 45 TFEU rights**: (a) accept offers of employment, (b) move freely for this purpose, (c) stay in a Member State for employment, (d) remain after employment (Regulation 492/2011)
3. Prohibit **discrimination on grounds of nationality** (Art. 45(2) TFEU): applies to access to employment, conditions of work, pay, dismissal, and social/tax advantages
4. Apply **Regulation 492/2011** (replacing Regulation 1612/68): equal treatment in employment conditions (Art. 7(1)), social advantages (Art. 7(2) — Cristini, C-32/75: fare reductions for large families), access to housing (Art. 9), education for children (Art. 10)
5. Apply **Bosman** (C-415/93): transfer rules restricting movement of professional sportspeople are caught by Art. 45; nationality quotas prohibited
6. Identify **indirectly discriminatory measures**: residency requirements, language requirements beyond what is necessary for the job (Groener, C-379/87 — Irish language for teaching in Ireland: justified)
7. Apply the **public service exception** (Art. 45(4) TFEU): narrowly construed — only posts involving exercise of public authority and safeguarding general interests of the State (Commission v Belgium, C-149/79)
8. Check **Directive 2004/38/EC** (Citizens' Rights Directive): right of residence beyond 3 months if worker; permanent residence after 5 years; family reunification rights (including third-country national family members)

### Verification
- Can identify a "worker" under EU law using Lawrie-Blum criteria
- Can detect direct and indirect nationality discrimination in employment
- Can advise on social advantages and residency rights under Regulation 492/2011

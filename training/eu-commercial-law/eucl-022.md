# EUCL-022 — EU Merger Control (Regulation 139/2004)

## Problema
Companies fail to identify notifiable concentrations or underestimate the Commission's power to block or condition mergers, risking gun-jumping fines and deal failure.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- EUCL-021 (dominance/market definition)

### Steps
1. Identify a **concentration** (Art. 3): (a) merger of previously independent undertakings, (b) acquisition of direct/indirect control (sole or joint), (c) creation of a full-function joint venture performing all functions of an autonomous economic entity on a lasting basis
2. Check **EU-dimension thresholds** (Art. 1):
   - Primary: combined worldwide turnover > EUR 5 billion AND EU-wide turnover of each of at least two parties > EUR 250 million — UNLESS each achieves more than 2/3 in a single Member State
   - Alternative (Art. 1(3)): combined worldwide > EUR 2.5 billion AND in each of at least 3 Member States combined > EUR 100 million, with each of at least two parties > EUR 25 million in each of those 3 states AND EU-wide each of at least two parties > EUR 100 million
3. **Notify the Commission** (Art. 4): mandatory pre-implementation notification using Form CO or Short Form CO (for simpler cases). **Standstill obligation** (Art. 7): cannot implement before clearance — gun-jumping fines up to 10% turnover
4. **Phase I review** (Art. 6): 25 working days. Outcomes: (a) clearance, (b) clearance with commitments, (c) serious doubts → proceed to Phase II
5. **Phase II investigation** (Art. 8): 90 working days (extendable). In-depth analysis. Outcomes: (a) clearance, (b) clearance with conditions/obligations, (c) prohibition
6. Apply the **SIEC test** (Art. 2): concentration prohibited if it would Significantly Impede Effective Competition, in particular through creation or strengthening of a dominant position. Assess: market shares, HHI, barriers to entry, countervailing buyer power, efficiencies, failing firm defence
7. **Remedies**: structural (divestiture — preferred) or behavioural (access commitments, firewalls). Offered by parties, accepted by Commission. Trustee monitors implementation
8. **Referral mechanisms**: Art. 9 (referral to Member State for distinct market), Art. 22 (Member State referral up to Commission — used for below-threshold "killer acquisitions" per 2021 Commission guidance), Art. 4(4)-(5) (pre-notification referral requests)

### Verification
- Can calculate whether EU-dimension thresholds are met
- Can identify the type of concentration (merger, acquisition of control, full-function JV)
- Can explain Phase I/II process and remedy types

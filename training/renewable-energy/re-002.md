# RE-002 — Measure and Calculate Electricity (kW, kWh, Capacity Factor)

## Problema
Confusion between power (kW) and energy (kWh) leads to incorrect project sizing and financial projections.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Basic understanding of electrical circuits

### Steps
1. Define and distinguish power (kW) vs energy (kWh)
2. Measure or obtain nameplate capacity of a generation asset
3. Record actual energy output over a defined period
4. Calculate capacity factor: actual output / (nameplate capacity x hours)
5. Compare capacity factors across technology types (solar ~20-25%, wind ~30-45%, nuclear ~90%)
6. Use capacity factor to estimate annual energy production

### Verification
- Verify capacity factor falls within expected range for technology type
- Cross-check kWh calculation: kW x hours = kWh

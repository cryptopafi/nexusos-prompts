# RE-001 — Calculate Energy Conversion Using SI Units and Thermodynamic Laws

## Problema
Practitioners lack fluency converting between energy units and applying thermodynamic principles, leading to errors in feasibility estimates.

## Procedura
### Prerequisites
- Access to SI unit reference tables and thermodynamic formulas

### Steps
1. Identify the energy forms involved (thermal, mechanical, electrical, chemical)
2. List all relevant SI units (J, W, kWh, BTU) and conversion factors
3. Apply the First Law of Thermodynamics to calculate energy input vs output
4. Apply the Second Law to determine maximum theoretical efficiency
5. Convert between unit systems using dimensional analysis
6. Calculate net energy output accounting for losses

### Verification
- Cross-check conversion results with an online energy unit calculator
- Verify thermodynamic calculations balance (energy in = energy out + losses)
